Bihar Vidhan Parishad: Supporting Upper House of Bihar Legislature

Transforming 24 Positions for Administrative Support in Bihar Legislative Council

The Bihar Vidhan Parishad Recruitment 2025 fills 24 Group D positions providing operational support to Bihar Legislative Council secretariat. This direct recruitment by Bihar Vidhan Parishad Sachivalaya offers stable government employment managing office operations, transport services, and daily administrative tasks for upper house of Bihar Legislature.

Post Distribution:

  • Driver: 9 posts (operating official vehicles, MLCs transport, secretariat vehicles)
  • Office Attendant (Parichari): 15 posts (office support, file carrying, cleaning, assistance)

Key Responsibilities:

  • Driver duties: Driving official vehicles for MLCs, officers, maintaining vehicles, fuel management
  • Attendant duties: Office cleaning, file distribution, serving tea/water, assisting officers
  • General support: Maintaining office premises, assisting in events, session duties

Salary and Benefits:

  • Office Attendant: ₹18,000-₹56,900 per month (Pay Level 1)
  • Driver: ₹19,900-₹63,200 per month (Pay Level 2)
  • Dearness Allowance: As per Bihar government rules
  • House Rent Allowance: Based on posting location

Vidhan Parishad staff enjoy government employee benefits including medical facilities, provident fund, pension after retirement, and job security working for prestigious legislative institution managing Bihar's legislative affairs and supporting elected members.

Selection Process: Written Exam and Practical Driving Test

OMR-Based Written Test with 100 Questions and 2 Hours Duration

The Bihar Vidhan Parishad selection 2025 follows transparent process combining written examination for both posts with additional practical driving test for driver candidates. Merit-based selection ensures qualified candidates join Bihar Legislative Council administrative cadre.

Written Examination Pattern:

  • Total questions: 100 MCQs (OMR-based objective type)
  • Total marks: 400 (each correct answer: 4 marks)
  • Negative marking: -1 mark for each wrong answer
  • Duration: 2 hours (120 minutes)
  • Language: Hindi/English (General Hindi section in Hindi only)

Subject-Wise Syllabus Distribution:

  • General Mathematics: LCM, HCF, fractions, decimals, percentage, profit-loss, ratio, simple/compound interest
  • General Knowledge: Indian history, geography, polity, Bihar GK, sports, awards, current affairs
  • General Science: Physics, chemistry, biology basics from 10th standard curriculum
  • General Hindi: Grammar, comprehension, vocabulary, sentence formation

Driving Test (For Driver Post):

  • Practical test: Driving different vehicle types (car, jeep, light commercial vehicles)
  • Skills assessed: Road safety, traffic rules, vehicle handling, parking
  • Qualifying nature: Must clear to be eligible for final selection
  • License verification: Valid LMV/HMV license mandatory

Application Process:

  • Registration: Online at blcsrecruitment.com (29 Sep - 20 Oct 2025)
  • Application fee: ₹100 for all categories (no exemption)
  • Payment: Online via debit/credit card, net banking, UPI
  • Documents: 10th certificate, driving license (for driver), photograph, signature

Final merit list prepared based on written exam marks for Office Attendant. For Driver post, written exam qualified candidates called for practical driving test with final merit combining both assessments ensuring competent vehicle operators.

Bihar Vidhan Parishad: Upper House of Bihar Legislature Since 1936

Supporting 75 MLCs Managing Legislative Affairs for 13 Crore Bihar Population

The Bihar Vidhan Parishad (Bihar Legislative Council) stands as upper house of bicameral Bihar Legislature with 75 MLCs (Members of Legislative Council) deliberating on legislation, reviewing bills, and contributing to governance. Administrative staff support this prestigious institution ensuring smooth functioning of democratic processes.

Vidhan Parishad Structure:

  • 75 MLCs: Elected/nominated members serving 6-year terms (staggered elections every 2 years)
  • Chairman: Presiding officer elected by MLCs (current tenure holder manages proceedings)
  • Legislative committees: Subject committees reviewing bills, policies, government functioning
  • Secretariat: Administrative wing with Secretary, officers, ministerial/Group D staff

Key Functions:

  • Legislation review: Examining bills passed by Vidhan Sabha (lower house)
  • Policy deliberation: Debating state policies, budget proposals, government schemes
  • Question hour: MLCs questioning ministers on government functioning
  • Committee work: Subject committees studying issues, submitting reports
  • Advisory role: Providing experienced perspective on legislative matters

Administrative Staff Role:

  • Driver responsibilities: Transporting MLCs to secretariat, residences, constituencies
  • Office Attendant duties: Maintaining offices, serving members during sessions, file management
  • Session support: Extra duties during monsoon/winter sessions (60-90 days annually)
  • Event assistance: Helping organize special sessions, conferences, dignitaries visits

Historical Significance:

  • Established: 1936 (among oldest legislative councils in India)
  • Abolition & revival: Abolished 1970, revived 2007 by Nitish Kumar government
  • Notable members: Former CMs, Union ministers, legal experts, social activists
  • Heritage building: Located in Patna's historic legislative complex near Gandhi Maidan

Recent Developments (2024-25):

  • Digital transformation: E-Vidhan Sabha app for paperless proceedings
  • Committee reforms: Enhanced subject committee functioning for effective scrutiny
  • Infrastructure upgrade: Modernization of chamber, committee rooms, MLC facilities
  • Transparency initiatives: Live streaming of proceedings, online question/answer database

Staff Welfare Measures:

  • Regular training: Workshops on parliamentary procedures, administrative efficiency
  • Performance incentives: Rewards for exemplary service during sessions
  • Welfare fund: Financial assistance during emergencies for staff and families
  • Housing support: Priority allotment in government housing schemes for Patna

Working at Bihar Vidhan Parishad offers unique opportunity to serve at heart of Bihar's legislative machinery, contributing to democratic processes while enjoying stable government employment with comprehensive benefits and exposure to state governance.
