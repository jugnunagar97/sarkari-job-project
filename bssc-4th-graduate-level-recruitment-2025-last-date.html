BSSC 4th Graduate Level: Gateway to Bihar Government Administrative Services

Transforming 1541 Graduates into Government Officers Across Multiple Bihar Departments

The BSSC 4th Graduate Level Combined Competitive Exam 2025 (also known as Bihar CGL) recruits graduates for Group C administrative positions across various Bihar government departments. This examination offers opportunity to serve in finance, planning, statistics, auditing, and industrial development departments managing state administration for 13 crore+ Bihar population.

Department-Wise Allocation:

  • Cooperative Department: 1,262 posts (Assistant Branch Officers in cooperative societies statewide)
  • Finance Department: 125 posts (Auditors handling state accounts, expenditure auditing)
  • Planning & Development: 88 posts (Planning Assistants for district/block level planning)
  • Industries Department: 60 posts (Industrial Officers for industrial promotion, MSME support)
  • Statistics Department: 5 posts (Junior Statistical Assistants for data analysis)
  • IT Department: 1 post (Data Entry Operator for digital record management)

Salary and Benefits:

  • Assistant Branch Officer/Planning Assistant: Pay Level 7 (₹44,900-₹1,42,400 per month)
  • Data Entry Operator: Pay Level 6 (₹35,400-₹1,12,400 per month)
  • Auditor/Cooperative Auditor: Pay Level 5 (₹29,200-₹92,300 per month)
  • Gross in-hand: ₹45,000-₹65,000/month initially (with DA, HRA, allowances)

BSSC CGL officers enjoy Bihar government benefits including medical facilities, provident fund, pension, leave travel concession, and promotion opportunities reaching Deputy Secretary/Joint Secretary level within 20-25 years ensuring stable career in state administration.

Two-Tier Selection: Prelims and Mains with No Interview for Graduate Level Posts

Pure Merit-Based Selection Through Computer-Based Examination in 2026

The BSSC 4th Graduate Level selection 2025 follows transparent two-stage process with Preliminary Examination (qualifying) followed by Main Examination (merit basis). No interview/personality test ensuring objective selection purely based on written examination performance minimizing subjectivity in recruitment process.

Stage I: Preliminary Examination (Qualifying):

  • Total questions: 150 MCQs (objective type, OMR-based)
  • Total marks: 150 (1 mark each question)
  • Duration: 2 hours 15 minutes
  • Subjects: General Studies (50Q), General Science (50Q), Mathematics (50Q)
  • Negative marking: -0.2 marks for each wrong answer
  • Qualifying nature: Only top candidates (15-20x vacancies) called for Mains

Stage II: Main Examination (Merit Basis):

  • Total questions: 150 MCQs (objective type, OMR-based)
  • Total marks: 300 (2 marks each question)
  • Duration: 2 hours 15 minutes
  • Paper I: General Studies (75Q, 150 marks—Indian history, geography, polity, Bihar GK)
  • Paper II: General Science & Mathematics (75Q, 150 marks—physics, chemistry, biology, maths)
  • Negative marking: -0.4 marks for each wrong answer

Final Merit Preparation:

  • Prelims marks: Not counted in final merit (qualifying only)
  • Final merit: Based solely on Main Examination marks out of 300
  • Service allocation: Based on merit rank and candidate's preference for posts/departments
  • Expected cutoffs: General (180-200/300), BC (160-175), SC/ST (140-160)

Application Process (Extended Deadline):

  • Registration: 25 August - 21 November 2025 (11:59 PM) at onlinebssc.com
  • Fee payment deadline: 21 November 2025
  • Final form submission: 24 November 2025 (after fee payment, edit/final submit)
  • Application fee: ₹100 for all categories (no exemption)
  • Documents: Upload photograph (10-100 KB), signature (5-50 KB), certificates

Candidates advised to prepare General Studies (50% weightage) focusing on Bihar GK, current affairs, and standard mathematics/science topics from graduation level curriculum ensuring balanced preparation for both examination stages.

Career Growth and Compensation in Bihar State Services

₹29,200-₹44,900 Starting Pay to ₹1,42,400 Maximum with State Government Perks

The BSSC Graduate Level career offers attractive Bihar state government salary with 7th Pay Commission benefits, regular increments, promotion opportunities, and comprehensive welfare schemes ensuring financially secure career serving Bihar's development initiatives across departments.

Post-Wise Initial Salary Breakup:

  • Assistant Branch Officer (Level 7): Basic ₹44,900 + DA ₹22,450 (50%) + HRA ₹4,490 (10%) = ₹71,840 gross/month
  • Data Entry Operator (Level 6): Basic ₹35,400 + DA ₹17,700 + HRA ₹3,540 = ₹56,640 gross/month
  • Auditor (Level 5): Basic ₹29,200 + DA ₹14,600 + HRA ₹2,920 = ₹46,720 gross/month
  • Annual increment: ₹1,300-₹1,800 in basic pay depending on pay level

Additional Allowances:

  • Dearness Allowance: 50% of basic pay (revised twice yearly by Bihar government)
  • House Rent Allowance: 10% in rural areas, 12% in urban areas
  • Transport Allowance: ₹1,200-₹1,600/month based on posting location
  • Children education allowance: ₹2,250/child/month (maximum 2 children)

Career Progression Path:

  • Assistant Branch Officer/Auditor (0-5 years): Entry-level administrative work in districts
  • Senior Assistant/Section Officer (5-10 years): Promotion via departmental exam (25% quota)
  • Deputy Superintendent (10-15 years): Supervisory roles in district offices
  • Superintendent/Assistant Director (15-20 years): Heading sections in directorate
  • Deputy Secretary/Joint Secretary (20-25 years): Senior policy-making positions

Comprehensive Benefits:

  • Medical facilities: Free treatment at government hospitals for self and family members
  • Leave benefits: 30 days earned leave + 8 days casual leave annually
  • Provident Fund: General Provident Fund with 7.1% annual interest
  • Pension: 50% of last drawn salary after 20 years service
  • Gratuity: ₹15-20 lakh on retirement (based on service years)
  • LTC: ₹40,000 biennial for family travel within India

Bihar government officers also eligible for housing loans at subsidized rates, vehicle loans, festival advances, and special allowances for working in Naxal-affected/backward districts ensuring comprehensive financial security throughout service career.

BSSC: Managing Bihar's Administrative Recruitment Since 1978

Conducting Transparent Examinations for 50,000+ Government Posts Annually

The Bihar Staff Selection Commission established in 1978 conducts competitive examinations for Group C and D positions across Bihar government departments. BSSC ensures merit-based transparent recruitment serving 38 government departments managing administration for India's third-most populous state with 13 crore+ population.

BSSC Examination Portfolio:

  • Graduate Level (CGL): Administrative posts requiring graduation (1,500-2,000 vacancies annually)
  • Inter Level: Clerical posts requiring 12th pass (3,000-4,000 vacancies annually)
  • Matriculation Level: Constables, attendants requiring 10th pass (5,000-10,000 vacancies)
  • Specialized recruitments: Teachers, technical staff, specialist positions (variable vacancies)

Major Departments Employing BSSC Graduates:

  • Cooperative Department: Largest employer (60% CGL posts) managing 8,000+ cooperative societies
  • Finance Department: Budget preparation, expenditure control, audit functions statewide
  • Planning & Development: District planning, MGNREGA implementation, rural development
  • Industries Department: Industrial promotion, MSME support, entrepreneurship development
  • Revenue Department: Land records, tax collection, district administration support

Work Profile for Graduate Level Officers:

  • Assistant Branch Officers: Managing cooperative society accounts, loan disbursement, auditing
  • Planning Assistants: Preparing district annual plans, monitoring scheme implementation
  • Auditors: Financial auditing, expenditure verification, maintaining government accounts
  • Industrial Officers: Promoting industries, facilitating MSME loans, monitoring industrial units
  • Statistical Assistants: Data collection, analysis, report preparation for government planning

Recent BSSC Achievements (2024):

  • Digital examination: 100% computer-based tests at 500+ centers across Bihar
  • Transparency initiatives: Online result declaration, scorecard download, grievance redressal
  • Fast-track recruitment: 25,000+ positions filled in 2023-24 (fastest in BSSC history)
  • Anti-cheating measures: IRIS scanning, facial recognition, AI-powered invigilation

Exam Statistics (3rd CGL 2022 Data):

  • Total applicants: 6.5 lakh (for 1,481 posts)
  • Prelims appeared: 3.8 lakh (58% attendance rate)
  • Prelims qualified: 25,000 candidates (6.6% qualification rate)
  • Mains appeared: 22,500 (90% conversion from prelims)
  • Finally selected: 1,481 officers (selection ratio 1:440 from total applicants)

Success Strategy for BSSC CGL:

  • Bihar GK focus: 25% questions from Bihar-specific topics (history, geography, current affairs)
  • NCERT mastery: Class 6-12 NCERT for General Science, History, Geography, Polity
  • Mathematics practice: Focus on arithmetic, algebra, geometry (graduation level difficulty)
  • Current affairs: Last 12 months national/Bihar news, government schemes, policies
  • Previous papers: Solving 10+ years' BSSC papers understanding question patterns
  • Mock tests: Regular full-length tests (50+ recommended) building speed and accuracy

BSSC Graduate Level remains Bihar's most sought-after state government recruitment offering job security, decent salary, promotion opportunities, and chance to contribute to Bihar's development across cooperative sector, finance, planning, and industrial sectors transforming state's administrative landscape.
