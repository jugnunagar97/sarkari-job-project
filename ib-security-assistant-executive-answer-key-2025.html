Intelligence Bureau Security Assistant: India's Internal Intelligence Force

Filling 4,987 Security Assistant Positions Serving India's Premier Intelligence Agency

Answer Key Analysis: Tier-I Exam Pattern and Expected Cutoffs

Provisional Answer Key Released on 4 October with Objection Window 4-7 October 2025

The IB Security Assistant Answer Key 2025 enables lakhs of candidates calculating expected scores comparing their OMR responses with official answers before result declaration. The answer key released for Tier-I Computer-Based Test conducted 29-30 September covering five subjects testing general awareness, quantitative aptitude, reasoning ability, English language, and general studies allowing candidates verifying their marked answers identifying discrepancies filing evidence-based objections during four-day window ensuring transparent fair evaluation process.

Tier-I Exam Pattern and Structure:

  • Total questions: 100 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) covering five subjects
  • Total marks: 100 marks (each question carries 1 mark)
  • Duration: 60 minutes (1 hour) for completing entire examination
  • General Awareness (20Q): Current affairs, Indian history, geography, polity, economy, science developments
  • Quantitative Aptitude (20Q): Arithmetic, algebra, geometry, data interpretation, numerical ability
  • Logical Reasoning & Analytical Ability (20Q): Series, patterns, analogies, coding-decoding, blood relations, directions
  • English Language (20Q): Grammar, vocabulary, comprehension, sentence improvement, error detection
  • General Studies (20Q): Indian culture, heritage, national movement, geography, environment, science & technology
  • Negative marking: 0.25 marks (1/4th mark) deducted for each wrong answer
  • Exam mode: Computer-based test conducted at authorized centers across India in multiple shifts

Marks Calculation Method:

  • Formula: Total Score = (Correct Answers × 1) - (Wrong Answers × 0.25)
  • Example: 75 correct + 15 wrong + 10 unattempted = (75 × 1) - (15 × 0.25) = 75 - 3.75 = 71.25 marks out of 100
  • Percentage calculation: (Total Score / 100) × 100 = Percentage achieved
  • Normalization: IB applies normalization formula for multi-shift exams adjusting scores based on difficulty levels

Expected Cutoff Marks (2025):

  • General/UR category: 68-75/100 (68-75% marks) based on normalized scores
  • OBC category: 63-70/100 (63-70% marks) with marginal relaxation
  • EWS category: 65-72/100 (65-72% marks) similar to general with slight variation
  • SC category: 55-62/100 (55-62% marks) with 10-13 marks relaxation
  • ST category: 50-57/100 (50-57% marks) with 15-18 marks relaxation from general
  • Minimum qualifying marks: UR/EWS (30%), OBC (28%), SC/ST (25%) as per official notification
  • Cutoff varies: Final cutoff depends on overall performance, difficulty level, vacancies, normalization factors

Objection Filing Process:

  • Objection window: 4-7 October 2025 (limited 4-day period for raising challenges)
  • Access portal: Visit official exam portal using login credentials (User ID and Password)
  • Question selection: Choose specific question number to challenge from displayed answer key
  • Evidence submission: Upload clear supporting documents from standard textbooks, official publications, reference materials
  • Valid references: Only objections with proper citations from NCERT, standard competitive exam books, government sources accepted
  • Objection fee: Pay prescribed fee per question (typically ₹100-₹200) through online payment gateway
  • Expert committee review: Subject experts evaluate each objection considering merit, evidence authenticity, academic validity
  • Fee refund: If objection accepted and answer key modified, full fee refunded within 6-8 weeks
  • Final answer key: Released after objection evaluation used for result preparation, no further appeals

Candidates scoring above expected cutoffs should prepare for Tier-II Descriptive Examination testing written communication skills, comprehension abilities, and subject knowledge ensuring readiness for next selection stage advancing toward IB Security Assistant appointment.

Career Progression: Security Work to Intelligence Operations Advancement

₹32,000 Starting Salary with Promotions to Higher Intelligence Cadres

The IB Security Assistant career offers structured progression path advancing from entry-level security operations to specialized intelligence functions. Security Assistants gain valuable field experience, develop operational skills, master intelligence protocols, and qualify for promotions to higher positions including Assistant Sub-Inspector (ASI) and Sub-Inspector (SI) levels managing intelligence operations serving India's premier internal security agency protecting nation from terrorism, espionage, and internal security threats.

Career Advancement Path:

  • Security Assistant (0-5 years): Entry level handling security duties, operational support, document management, field assistance
  • Senior Security Assistant (5-10 years): Promotion through departmental exams managing junior staff, handling complex operations
  • Assistant Sub-Inspector (10-15 years): Supervisory role coordinating security operations, intelligence gathering, team management
  • Sub-Inspector (15-20 years): Field intelligence operations, investigation assistance, operational planning, inter-agency coordination
  • Inspector (20-25 years): Senior operational roles managing intelligence units, strategic planning, mentoring junior officers

Job Responsibilities and Work Environment:

  • Security operations: Physical security of IB installations, access control, visitor management, perimeter security
  • Intelligence support: Assisting field officers, maintaining surveillance, information collection, ground-level intelligence
  • Operational duties: Accompanying officers during field operations, crowd monitoring, event security, VIP protection
  • Communication tasks: Operating secure communication systems, message handling, coordination with other agencies
  • Administrative work: Record maintenance, report preparation, logistics support, inventory management
  • Training programs: Regular training in intelligence operations, security protocols, communication systems, field craft

Comprehensive Benefits Package:

  • Basic salary: ₹21,700-₹69,100/month (Pay Level 3) with regular annual increments
  • Dearness Allowance: 50% of basic salary (revised biannually based on inflation index)
  • House Rent Allowance: 8-24% of basic depending on posting location (X, Y, Z class cities)
  • Transport Allowance: ₹1,600-₹3,600/month for commuting expenses
  • Intelligence duty allowance: Special allowance for operational fieldwork, sensitive assignments
  • Risk allowance: Additional compensation for high-risk operations, counter-terrorism assignments
  • Medical facilities: CGHS coverage + reimbursement + cashless hospitalization for self and family
  • GPF accumulation: General Provident Fund with 7.1% interest building retirement corpus
  • Pension: Monthly pension after retirement ensuring post-retirement financial security
  • LTC: Leave Travel Concession for family travel covering transportation costs

Work Culture and Environment:

  • Working hours: Shift-based duties (8-hour shifts) with rotating schedule ensuring 24x7 security coverage
  • Posting locations: All-India service with postings across state capitals, major cities, sensitive border areas
  • Transfer policy: Transfers every 3-5 years ensuring diverse experience, operational exposure across regions
  • Confidentiality: Strict security protocols, confidentiality agreements, limited disclosure about work nature
  • Physical fitness: Regular fitness tests, training programs maintaining operational readiness

IB Security Assistant positions provide rewarding careers serving nation's premier intelligence agency, gaining valuable operational experience, enjoying Central Government benefits, job security, promotion opportunities to intelligence cadres making security service attractive profession for 10th pass candidates aspiring government jobs with responsibility and purpose.

Intelligence Bureau: India's Oldest Intelligence Agency Since 1887

Managing Internal Security, Counter-Terrorism, and Intelligence Operations Nationwide

The Intelligence Bureau established in 1887 serves as India's oldest and premier internal intelligence agency operating under Ministry of Home Affairs. IB manages internal security intelligence, counter-terrorism operations, counter-espionage activities, VIP security coordination, and threat assessment protecting India from internal security threats, terrorist activities, espionage attempts ensuring national sovereignty, integrity, and territorial security serving 140 crore citizens across diverse threat landscape.

IB Core Functions:

  • Counter-terrorism: Intelligence gathering on terrorist organizations, threat assessment, preventive actions, coordination with state agencies
  • Counter-espionage: Detecting foreign intelligence activities, preventing espionage, protecting sensitive installations, information security
  • Internal security: Monitoring extremist activities, communal tensions, border security threats, law and order intelligence
  • VIP security: Coordinating security for Prime Minister, President, other VVIPs, threat perception reports
  • Cyber intelligence: Monitoring online threats, cyber terrorism, social media intelligence, digital forensics
  • Aviation security: Airport security coordination, passenger profiling, aviation threat assessment

Organizational Structure:

  • Headquarters: New Delhi with Director as head reporting to Union Home Minister
  • Regional offices: Subsidiary Intelligence Bureaus (SIBs) across all states and union territories
  • Special branches: Technical intelligence, counter-intelligence, internal security, VIP security divisions
  • Field units: District-level presence ensuring grassroots intelligence collection, local coordination
  • Total strength: 25,000+ personnel including intelligence officers, technical staff, security assistants across cadres
