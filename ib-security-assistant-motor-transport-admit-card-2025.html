Understanding IB Security Assistant Motor Transport Selection Process and Examination Structure

Complete Guide to Intelligence Bureau Motor Transport Recruitment Stages and Assessment Framework

The IB Security Assistant Motor Transport selection process follows comprehensive two-tier assessment framework beginning with Computer Based Test Tier-I scheduled for 30 October 2025 comprising objective-type multiple choice questions covering General Awareness about Indian history, geography, polity, economy, current affairs, Reasoning Ability testing logical thinking and problem-solving skills, Quantitative Aptitude assessing numerical abilities and mathematical calculations, and English Language evaluating grammar, vocabulary, comprehension, and communication skills. The computer-based examination with negative marking penalizing incorrect responses ensures comprehensive candidate evaluation identifying intellectually capable individuals suitable for intelligence agency employment.

Successful Tier-I candidates advance to Tier-II comprising Driving Test evaluating practical driving skills on various road conditions, traffic situations, and vehicle handling capabilities assessing competence in operating light motor vehicles safely and efficiently. This practical assessment tests candidates' driving proficiency, road sense, adherence to traffic rules, vehicle control, and ability to handle emergency situations demonstrating skills essential for motorized transport duties. Selected candidates undergo Interview assessing personality, communication abilities, motivation, and suitability for intelligence agency service before document verification confirming educational certificates, driving license validity, experience proof, and identity documents preceding medical examination ensuring fitness for driving duties.

Selection Process Breakdown:

  • Tier-I CBT: Computer-based objective test covering general awareness, reasoning, quantitative aptitude, and English language with negative marking ensuring comprehensive intellectual assessment suitable for government service
  • Tier-II Driving Test: Practical assessment evaluating driving skills, vehicle handling, traffic rule adherence, road safety awareness, and ability to operate light motor vehicles professionally across diverse conditions
  • Interview: Personal interaction assessing communication skills, personality traits, motivation for joining Intelligence Bureau, understanding of job responsibilities, and overall suitability for intelligence agency employment
  • Document Verification: Scrutiny of matriculation certificates, valid LMV driving license, one year driving experience proof, domicile certificate, age proof, and identity documents confirming eligibility compliance

Final merit lists are prepared based on combined performance across Tier-I examination and Tier-II driving test with interview providing additional assessment inputs. Selected candidates undergo comprehensive medical examination assessing visual acuity, hearing, cardiovascular fitness, and overall health ensuring physical fitness for driving duties before receiving appointment letters for training at Intelligence Bureau facilities.
