The IBPS RRB 14th Correction Window, open from 06-07 October 2025, represents a crucial opportunity for candidates who submitted their applications with errors or incomplete information. This two-day edit window enables applicants to rectify mistakes in their application forms for 13,294 positions across Office Assistant and Officer Scale posts in Regional Rural Banks nationwide.

The correction facility requires payment of â‚¹200 non-refundable fee applicable to all categories. Candidates must understand this represents their only opportunity to modify application details before final processing commences. Missing this window could result in disqualification due to incorrect information preventing candidates from appearing in examinations scheduled for November-December 2025.

Key Correction Window Features:

  • Two-day correction period from 06-07 October 2025 providing limited timeframe for modifications and updates
  • One-time correction opportunity meaning candidates cannot make multiple attempts requiring careful review before submission
  • Non-refundable correction fee of â‚¹200 applicable to all categories ensuring candidates verify changes thoroughly before payment

Understanding IBPS RRB Correction Process and Editable Fields

Complete Guide to IBPS RRB Application Correction Procedure and Restrictions

The IBPS RRB correction process follows systematic approach enabling candidates to access their submitted applications through registration credentials. After login, candidates view their complete application with editable and non-editable fields clearly distinguished. The system highlights fields eligible for modification while restricting critical identification details preventing fraudulent changes post submission.

Editable fields include personal details like date of birth, father's name, category selection, educational qualification details, experience information for Officer Scale posts, photograph and signature uploads, and correspondence address excluding state selection. Candidates must carefully review each editable field comparing with original documents before initiating corrections.

Correction Window Guidelines:

  • Login Credentials: Use original registration number and password/date of birth exactly as entered during initial application submission
  • Field Restrictions: Name, nationality, email ID, mobile number, permanent address state, correspondence address state, and post selection cannot be modified
  • Payment Process: Correction fee of â‚¹200 must be paid through online modes before final submission of corrected application
  • Verification Process: Thoroughly verify all corrected details matching with original certificates before clicking final submit button

After successful submission, candidates must download and print the corrected application form maintaining copies for future reference during admit card download, examination day, document verification, and interview stages. The correction acknowledgment serves as proof of updated information superseding original application details for all subsequent recruitment stages.

Strategic Approach to IBPS RRB Application Correction and Common Mistakes

Essential Tips for Error-Free Correction and Avoiding Common Application Pitfalls

The IBPS RRB correction window requires strategic approach beginning with thorough review of original application identifying all errors requiring correction. Common mistakes include incorrect date of birth entries, wrong category selection affecting age relaxation and fee payment, educational qualification percentage errors, improper photograph/signature specifications, and experience certificate mismatches for Officer Scale II and III positions.

Candidates must gather all original documents including mark sheets, degree certificates, category certificates, and experience letters before initiating correction process. Cross-verify each detail in application against original documents ensuring 100% accuracy. Particular attention should focus on educational qualification percentages, experience duration calculations, and category certificate validity dates directly impacting eligibility verification.

Common Correction Errors:

  • Date of Birth Mismatch: Verify date of birth matches exactly with Class 10 certificate format avoiding future complications during verification
  • Category Certificate Issues: Ensure category selected matches certificate issued by competent authority with valid dates covering recruitment period
  • Photograph/Signature Specifications: Upload images meeting exact size, format, and quality requirements specified avoiding rejection during verification stages
  • Educational Percentage Errors: Calculate aggregate percentage correctly including all subjects as per university marking scheme avoiding eligibility issues

Candidates should avoid last-minute corrections on 07 October 2025 preventing technical issues or payment gateway failures. Complete correction process preferably on 06 October 2025 allowing sufficient time for careful review and successful submission. Maintain screenshot evidence of successful correction submission including payment confirmation for personal records and dispute resolution purposes.
