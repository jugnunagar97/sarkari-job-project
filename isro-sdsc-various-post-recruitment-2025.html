ISRO SDSC Various Post Recruitment 2025 – Apply Online Now

Post Date: October 16, 2025 | 3:46 PM IST

The Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO), Satish Dhawan Space Centre (SDSC) has officially released the notification for Various Post Recruitment 2025 on its official website. This prestigious recruitment offers 141 positions across multiple disciplines including Engineers, Technicians, Nurses, Firemen, Cooks, and Drivers. The online application process started on 16 October 2025 and will continue until 14 November 2025. Candidates aged 18-35 years as on 14 November 2025 possessing relevant qualifications (B.E./B.Tech, Diploma, ITI, or equivalent certifications) are eligible to apply. Application fee is ₹750 for General/OBS/EWS and ₹500 for SC/ST/PWD candidates. Selection includes Written Examination, Physical Tests, Skill Tests, Document Verification, and Medical Examination. This represents exceptional opportunity to join India's premier space research organization (Advt. No. SDSC SHAR/RMT/01/2025).

How to Fill ISRO SDSC Various Post Online Form 2025
  • Visit official ISRO SDSC online application portal at https://apps.shar.gov.in/Recruitment01_2025/main.jsp.
  • Click on "Apply Online" or registration option for new users.
  • Create account with valid email ID and mobile number providing personal information.
  • Verify email address and mobile number through OTP for account security.
  • Log in using your credentials to access ISRO SDSC recruitment application portal.
  • Select preferred post category matching your qualifications and experience.
  • Fill all mandatory personal details including name, date of birth, father's name, address carefully.
  • Provide complete educational qualification details matching post requirements exactly.
  • Mention relevant work experience for positions requiring experience (Cook, Driver, etc.).
  • Upload recent passport-sized photograph (4.5cm x 3.5cm, JPG format, white background) ensuring clarity.
  • Upload signature in specified dimensions (3cm x 1.5cm, JPG format) using black/blue ink on white paper.
  • Select preferred examination center from available ISRO SDSC locations across India.
  • Review all entered information thoroughly checking against original documents.
  • Proceed to payment section and pay applicable examination fee through available online methods.
  • After successful payment, save and print application confirmation with registration number.
  • Deadline: 14 November 2025 – Apply immediately to avoid last-minute delays or technical issues.
ISRO SDSC Various Post Recruitment 2025: Selection Process
  • Written Examination: Objective-type test evaluating general knowledge, technical subject knowledge, English proficiency, reasoning, and professional awareness relevant to specific position
  • Physical Test: For positions like Fireman and Driver - Physical efficiency test including running, jumping, climbing, and strength assessment meeting standards
  • Skill Test: Practical skill evaluation for technical positions assessing hands-on competence in relevant trade/discipline under timed conditions
  • Document Verification: Comprehensive verification of educational certificates, qualification documents, age proof, experience certificates, identity documents ensuring eligibility
  • Medical Examination: Complete medical fitness assessment by authorized ISRO medical practitioners ensuring candidates meet prescribed health standards
  • Final Merit List: Prepared based on written exam performance, physical/skill test scores, document verification clearance ensuring only eligible qualified candidates selected

ISRO Functions, Space Missions, and Organizational Significance

Understanding ISRO's Role in India's Space Program and Scientific Research

The Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO), established as India's national space agency, leads the nation's space exploration, satellite technology, and scientific research programs. ISRO operates multiple launch centers including Satish Dhawan Space Centre (SDSC) at Sriharikota, India's primary spaceport conducting satellite launches, spacecraft missions, and national space programs. The organization manages diverse missions including earth observation satellites, communication satellites, navigation systems (NavIC), lunar missions (Chandrayaan), Mars missions (Mangalyaan), and solar research (Aditya-L1) establishing India as spacefaring nation contributing global scientific knowledge. SDSC-SHAR coordinates launch operations, testing facilities, ground support systems, and technical operations ensuring successful mission execution. ISRO's workforce combines scientists, engineers, technicians, and support staff working collaboratively advancing India's technological capabilities and contributing international space exploration.

ISRO's Strategic Mission Areas:

  • Satellite Launches: Regular launch operations deploying communication, earth observation, and navigation satellites advancing national objectives
  • Space Exploration: Lunar missions, interplanetary exploration, scientific missions advancing human knowledge and technological frontiers
  • Communication Systems: Satellite communications supporting telecommunications, disaster management, and national connectivity
  • Earth Observation: Remote sensing satellites monitoring agriculture, disasters, environmental changes, and urban development
  • Navigation Services: NavIC system providing precise positioning supporting transportation, power systems, and telecommunications
  • Scientific Research: Advanced research in propulsion, materials, electronics supporting space vehicle development

Career Impact and National Contribution: ISRO employees contribute directly to India's space exploration, technological advancement, and national security objectives. Scientists and engineers develop cutting-edge technologies enabling ambitious space missions launching India's presence globally. Technicians ensure reliable operations maintaining complex launch facilities and ground systems enabling successful missions. Medical and support staff ensure operational readiness and personnel welfare. Throughout careers at ISRO, professionals gain exposure to advanced technology, international collaborations, and meaningful contribution advancing India's scientific capabilities. The organization provides lifetime government employment with pension security, comprehensive benefits, continuous professional development, and lasting impact on India's technological development and space exploration throughout distinguished careers in the nation's premier space research organization leading humanity's understanding of the cosmos.
