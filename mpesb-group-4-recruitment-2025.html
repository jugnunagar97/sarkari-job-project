Stenographer Duties, Computer Skills, and Administrative Functions

Understanding Stenographer Role and Technical Competency Requirements

The MPESB Group 4 Stenographer position involves comprehensive stenographic, administrative, and clerical responsibilities supporting government officials and departments. Stenographers maintain efficient shorthand documentation services during meetings, dictations, official proceedings while ensuring accuracy and confidentiality. The role requires proficiency in shorthand systems, typing speed meeting government standards, English and Hindi language competence, and computer literacy in MS Office, email communication, and digital documentation. Stenographers manage administrative tasks including file organization, correspondence handling, official documentation preparation, and record management. The examination pattern emphasizes Mathematics, General Knowledge, Hindi, English, and Computer knowledge reflecting modern government administrative demands.

Key Stenographer Responsibilities:

  • Shorthand Documentation: Record speeches, dictations, meetings, official proceedings using stenography
  • Transcription Services: Convert recorded shorthand and dictations into typed documents accurately
  • Administrative Support: Provide comprehensive support to government officers and departments
  • Computer Operations: MS Office, email, digital documentation, database management
  • Record Management: Maintain organized filing systems, official archives, secure documentation
  • Hindi/English Communication: Official correspondence, document drafting in both languages
