AISSEE 2026: Two-Tier Admission Process with Written Exam and Medical Fitness

Comprehensive Assessment Testing Academic Knowledge, Intelligence, and Physical Fitness for Military Education

The AISSEE 2026 selection process follows systematic approach combining written entrance examination testing academic subjects and intelligence quotient followed by medical examination ensuring physical fitness, document verification authenticating credentials, and online counselling for school allotment based on merit rank and preference ensuring transparent, merit-based admission to prestigious Sainik Schools nationwide.

Written Examination Pattern (Class VI):

  • Total marks: 300 (300 questions, each 1 mark)
  • Duration: 150 minutes (2 hours 30 minutes)
  • Mathematics (100 marks): Arithmetic, geometry, mensuration, data handling (Class V NCERT level)
  • Intelligence (50 marks): Pattern recognition, series completion, logical reasoning, spatial reasoning
  • Language (100 marks): English (50 marks)â€”grammar, vocabulary, comprehension; Hindi (50 marks)â€”similar pattern
  • General Knowledge (50 marks): History, geography, civics, current affairs, sports, science facts
  • Medium: Available in 13 languages (Hindi, English, Punjabi, Bengali, Tamil, Telugu, Marathi, Gujarati, Malayalam, Kannada, Odia, Assamese, Urdu)
  • No negative marking: Every question carries 1 mark, wrong answer gives zero marks

Written Examination Pattern (Class IX):

  • Total marks: 400 (400 questions, each 1 mark)
  • Duration: 180 minutes (3 hours)
  • Mathematics (100 marks): Algebra, geometry, trigonometry, mensuration (Class VIII NCERT level)
  • Intelligence (50 marks): Analytical reasoning, logical deduction, pattern recognition, problem-solving
  • English (100 marks): Grammar, vocabulary, comprehension passages, sentence formation, writing skills
  • General Science (100 marks): Physics, chemistry, biology concepts (Class VIII NCERT)
  • Social Studies (50 marks): History, geography, civics, economics (Class VIII NCERT)
  • Medium: English only (no regional language option for Class IX)
  • No negative marking: Encourages attempting all questions without penalty for wrong answers

Seat Allocation and Reservation:

  • 40% All India Merit: 40% seats filled purely on merit basis without state/category reservation
  • 60% State Quota: 60% seats reserved for state domicile candidates with category reservations
  • Category reservation: SC (15%), ST (7.5%), OBC (27%), EWS (10%), Defence (25% in state quota)
  • Home State advantage: Higher chances for candidates from school's state due to state quota

Medical Examination Standards:

  • Physical fitness: Height, weight, chest measurements as per age-specific norms
  • Vision test: 6/6 vision without glasses preferred, glasses permitted within prescribed limits
  • General health: No chronic diseases, physical deformities, dental issues affecting fitness
  • Medical board: Conducted by authorized medical authorities at designated centers
  • Disqualification factors: Flat foot, knock knees, severe visual impairment, hearing defects

Expected Cutoffs (Based on Previous Trends):

  • Class VI General: 200-220/300 (67-73%)
  • Class VI Reserved: 180-200/300 (60-67%)
  • Class IX General: 260-280/400 (65-70%)
  • Class IX Reserved: 240-260/400 (60-65%)
  • Cutoff varies: School-wise, state-wise cutoffs differ based on demand and seat availability

Preparation Strategy:

  • NCERT books: Thoroughly study Class V (for Class VI exam) and Class VIII (for Class IX exam) NCERT textbooks
  • Previous papers: Solve last 5 years AISSEE papers understanding question patterns and difficulty level
  • Mock tests: Regular practice tests (30-40 recommended) building speed and exam temperament
  • Intelligence section: Practice reasoning books (RS Aggarwal, Arihant) developing logical thinking skills
  • General knowledge: Read newspapers, current affairs magazines, GK books covering diverse topics
  • Time management: Practice completing entire paper in stipulated time ensuring all questions attempted

Candidates advised to focus on conceptual clarity rather than rote learning, develop strong foundation in mathematics and science, enhance English language skills through reading and writing practice, and maintain regular study schedule ensuring comprehensive preparation for competitive entrance examination.
