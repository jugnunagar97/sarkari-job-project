How To Fill NTA CMAT 2026 Online Application Form
  • Visit official National Testing Agency website at cmat.nta.nic.in or examinationservices.nic.in portal.
  • Click on "CMAT 2026" or "Apply Online" button prominently displayed on homepage.
  • For new registration, click "New Candidate Registration" entering basic details including name, email ID, mobile number.
  • Verify email and mobile number through OTP sent for authentication and account creation purposes.
  • After successful registration, login using credentials (application number and password) received via SMS/email.
  • Fill complete application form including personal details, academic qualifications, address, category information carefully.
  • Upload recent passport size photograph (JPG/JPEG, 10-200 KB) and signature (JPG/JPEG, 4-30 KB) as per specifications.
  • Select preferred exam city from dropdown list (multiple city preferences can be selected for better allocation).
  • Review all entered information thoroughly checking for any errors, omissions before proceeding to payment.
  • Pay application fee online using debit card, credit card, net banking, UPI as per your category.
  • General category candidates pay ₹2,500, while OBC/EWS/SC/ST/PH/Female candidates pay ₹1,250 only.
  • Submit application form after successful payment and take printout for future reference purposes.
  • Note down application number and keep payment receipt safe for admit card download later.
  • Complete application process before deadline 17 November 2025 (11:50 PM) avoiding last-minute technical issues.

CMAT: Gateway to 1,300+ MBA Colleges Across India

National Level Management Entrance Exam for MBA/PGDM Admissions 2026-27

The Common Management Admission Test (CMAT) 2026 serves as national-level entrance examination conducted by National Testing Agency for admission to MBA and PGDM programmes in 1,300+ AICTE-approved institutions across India for Academic Session 2026-27. CMAT evaluates candidates' aptitude across five key areas including Quantitative Techniques and Data Interpretation, Logical Reasoning, Language Comprehension, General Awareness, and Innovation & Entrepreneurship through computer-based test format ensuring standardized assessment. Top MBA colleges including JBIMS Mumbai, K J Somaiya, Welingkar Institute, Great Lakes Chennai, BIMTECH, Goa Institute of Management, and numerous university departments, constituent colleges, and affiliated colleges accept CMAT scores for admission providing aspiring managers access to quality management education, industry exposure, placement opportunities, and career advancement in business, finance, marketing, operations, human resources, and entrepreneurship domains contributing India's business leadership development.

CMAT 2026 Exam Highlights:

  • Conducting body: National Testing Agency (NTA) under Ministry of Education, Government of India
  • Exam level: National level entrance examination accepted by AICTE-approved institutions nationwide
  • Exam mode: Computer Based Test (CBT) conducted at designated test centers across India
  • Exam duration: 180 minutes (3 hours) for completing 100 multiple choice questions
  • Total questions: 100 MCQs divided equally into five sections (20 questions each section)
  • Total marks: 400 marks with each question carrying 4 marks for correct answer
  • Negative marking: 1 mark deducted for each incorrect answer (no negative marking for unattempted)
  • Exam language: English only for all sections ensuring standardized assessment nationwide
  • No sectional time limit: Candidates can attempt sections in any order managing time efficiently
  • Accepting colleges: 1,300+ AICTE-approved B-schools across India for MBA/PGDM admissions

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Educational qualification: Bachelor's degree (graduation) in any discipline from recognized university in India
  • Minimum percentage: No minimum aggregate marks required in graduation for CMAT eligibility
  • Final year students: Candidates appearing in final year of bachelor's degree can also apply
  • Age limit: No age bar for CMAT examination - candidates at any career stage can appear
  • Nationality: Indian nationals, NRI, PIO, OCI candidates eligible for CMAT examination
  • College-specific criteria: Individual MBA colleges may have minimum graduation percentage (50% or 45%) for admissions
Section-Wise Syllabus: Comprehensive Coverage for Management Aptitude

Five Sections Testing Quantitative, Logical, Verbal, Awareness, and Innovation Skills

The CMAT 2026 Syllabus comprehensively covers five distinct sections each containing 20 questions testing different management aptitude skills. Quantitative Techniques and Data Interpretation section evaluates mathematical problem-solving abilities covering arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and data analysis. Logical Reasoning tests analytical thinking through puzzles, patterns, and deductive reasoning. Language Comprehension assesses English proficiency including vocabulary, grammar, reading comprehension, and verbal ability. General Awareness evaluates knowledge of current affairs, business, economy, sports, and general knowledge. Innovation & Entrepreneurship section unique to CMAT tests understanding of entrepreneurial concepts, innovation principles, business models, and startup ecosystems preparing candidates for modern business leadership roles requiring creative problem-solving and entrepreneurial mindset.

Section 1: Quantitative Techniques & Data Interpretation (20 Questions)

  • Arithmetic: Number systems, percentages, profit-loss, simple-compound interest, ratio-proportion, averages, partnerships
  • Algebra: Linear equations, quadratic equations, inequalities, logarithms, progressions (AP, GP)
  • Geometry: Lines, angles, triangles, circles, mensuration (area, volume, perimeter calculations)
  • Trigonometry: Trigonometric ratios, identities, heights and distances applications
  • Data Interpretation: Tables, bar graphs, line graphs, pie charts, data sufficiency questions
  • Modern Math: Permutations-combinations, probability, statistics (mean, median, mode, standard deviation)

Section 2: Logical Reasoning (20 Questions)

  • Analytical reasoning: Coding-decoding, blood relations, direction sense, seating arrangements, syllogisms
  • Pattern recognition: Number series, letter series, analogy, odd one out, classification
  • Critical thinking: Cause and effect, assumptions, conclusions, arguments, inferences
  • Puzzles: Linear arrangements, circular arrangements, scheduling, grouping problems
  • Data sufficiency: Determining adequacy of data for solving problems requiring logical analysis

Section 3: Language Comprehension (20 Questions)

  • Reading comprehension: Passages with questions on main idea, inference, tone, vocabulary in context
  • Grammar: Subject-verb agreement, tenses, articles, prepositions, parts of speech, sentence correction
  • Vocabulary: Synonyms, antonyms, word meanings, idioms and phrases, one-word substitutions
  • Verbal ability: Para jumbles, sentence completion, fill in blanks, error detection
  • Writing skills: Sentence improvement, para formation, coherence and consistency

Section 4: General Awareness (20 Questions)

  • Current affairs: National and international events from past 6-12 months including major developments
  • Business & economy: Stock markets, banking, fiscal policy, budget, economic indicators, major deals
  • Sports: Major tournaments, winners, records, awards, international competitions
  • Awards & honors: Nobel prizes, Padma awards, Booker prize, other prestigious recognitions
  • Books & authors: Recent publications, bestsellers, important literary works
  • Static GK: Indian geography, history, polity, culture, important personalities, organizations

Section 5: Innovation & Entrepreneurship (20 Questions)

  • Innovation concepts: Types of innovation, innovation process, disruptive innovation, technological advancement
  • Entrepreneurship fundamentals: Characteristics of entrepreneurs, business opportunity identification, risk management
  • Business models: Traditional and modern business models, revenue streams, value proposition
  • Startup ecosystem: Startup India initiative, funding stages, venture capital, angel investors, incubators
  • IPR & Patents: Intellectual Property Rights, patents, trademarks, copyrights in business context
  • Case studies: Success stories of Indian and global entrepreneurs, startup unicorns, business strategies
Preparation Strategy: Scoring High in CMAT 2026

Comprehensive Study Plan Covering All Sections with Mock Tests and Time Management

The CMAT 2026 Preparation requires systematic approach covering all five sections with emphasis on conceptual clarity, practice, and time management. Candidates should dedicate 3-4 months for comprehensive preparation starting with understanding syllabus thoroughly. Quantitative and Logical Reasoning require regular practice solving diverse problems. Language Comprehension improves through reading newspapers, articles, and practicing grammar exercises. General Awareness demands daily news reading focusing on business, economy, and current affairs. Innovation & Entrepreneurship section requires studying startup ecosystems, business models, and entrepreneurial case studies. Mock tests are crucial for time management, identifying weak areas, and building exam temperament. With 180 minutes for 100 questions, candidates must maintain pace of approximately 1.8 minutes per question while ensuring accuracy to maximize score considering negative marking of 1 mark per wrong answer.

Month-Wise Study Plan:

  • Month 1: Complete syllabus coverage - study all topics systematically, make notes, understand concepts thoroughly
  • Month 2: Topic-wise practice - solve chapter-wise questions, previous year papers, strengthen weak areas
  • Month 3: Sectional tests - take section-wise timed tests, improve speed and accuracy, revise concepts
  • Month 4: Full-length mocks - attempt 20-25 full mock tests, analyze performance, work on time management
  • Final week: Revision - quick revision of formulas, shortcuts, current affairs, previous mistakes analysis

Section-Wise Preparation Tips:

  • Quantitative Techniques: Learn formulas, shortcuts, practice 50+ questions daily, focus on speed and accuracy
  • Logical Reasoning: Practice diverse puzzle types, develop pattern recognition, work on analytical thinking
  • Language Comprehension: Read English newspapers daily, practice 10-15 RC passages, improve vocabulary systematically
  • General Awareness: Read business newspapers (Economic Times, Business Standard), follow major events, make monthly notes
  • Innovation & Entrepreneurship: Study Startup India portal, read entrepreneurship books, follow startup news, analyze case studies

Recommended Study Materials:

  • Quantitative Aptitude: R.S. Aggarwal, Arun Sharma books covering arithmetic, algebra, geometry comprehensively
  • Logical Reasoning: Arun Sharma LR book, practice puzzles from various sources, previous year CMAT papers
  • Verbal Ability: Norman Lewis Word Power Made Easy, Wren & Martin grammar, reading comprehension practice books
  • General Awareness: Daily newspapers, Pratiyogita Darpan, monthly current affairs magazines, Manorama Yearbook
  • Mock Tests: NTA official mock test, major test series (TIME, IMS, Career Launcher, Oliveboard)

Time Management Strategy:

  • Section allocation: Allocate 30-35 minutes per section based on difficulty and personal strength
  • Easy questions first: Attempt easy questions first ensuring maximum accuracy in comfortable areas
  • Skip difficult ones: Don't waste time on very difficult questions - mark for review, attempt if time permits
  • Negative marking awareness: Avoid random guessing - attempt only when 70-80% confident about answer
  • Review time: Keep 10-15 minutes for reviewing marked questions and ensuring all sections attempted
NTA: Conducting Transparent National Level Entrance Examinations

Government Agency Ensuring Fair Assessment for 50+ Lakh Candidates Annually

The National Testing Agency (NTA) established 2017 operates as autonomous organization under Ministry of Education conducting efficient, transparent, and international standards-based testing for admission to higher education institutions. NTA conducts major entrance examinations including JEE Main, NEET, CUET, CMAT, GPAT, and various other tests serving 50+ lakh candidates annually. With advanced technology infrastructure, secure examination delivery systems, and computerized test centers across India, NTA ensures fair assessment maintaining examination integrity through biometric authentication, CCTV surveillance, and stringent security protocols. CMAT conducted by NTA since 2019 has emerged as widely accepted MBA entrance exam providing standardized evaluation platform for business schools ensuring merit-based admissions contributing quality management education ecosystem supporting India's business leadership development vision preparing competent managers for corporate sector, entrepreneurship, and public administration roles.
