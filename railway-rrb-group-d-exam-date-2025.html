RRB GROUP D RECRUITMENT 2025 : ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

To be eligible for the RRB Group D Recruitment Exam, candidates must have completed Class 10 (High School) from a school recognized by NCVT/SCVT or possess a National Apprenticeship Certificate (NAC) issued by NCVT. Railway RRB Group D Exam 2025: FAQs

Question: What are the announced dates for the Railway RRB Group D Exam 2025?

Answer: The Computer-Based Test (CBT) for the RRB Group D Level-1 posts is officially scheduled to be held in multiple phases from November 17, 2025, and will continue until the end of December 2025. This recruitment drive, under CEN-08/2024, aims to fill a total of 32,438 vacancies across various departments of Indian Railways.

Question: What is the exam schedule and format for the RRB Group D CBT?

Answer: The CBT will be conducted in various shifts across different zones to accommodate the large number of applicants. The test will last for 90 minutes and will consist of objective-type questions covering four subjects: General Science, Mathematics, General Awareness, and General Intelligence & Reasoning. It's important to note that there will be a negative marking of 1/3 mark for every incorrect answer.

Question: When will the admit card and city intimation slip be released?

Answer: Candidates should regularly check their respective regional RRB websites for updates. The exam city intimation slip, which helps candidates with travel plans, will be issued approximately 10 days before the exam date. The admit card, or hall ticket, will be available for download four days before the scheduled exam date. Both documents are essential for entry into the examination center.

Question: What are the stages of the RRB Group D 2025 selection process?

Answer: The selection process for this recruitment is divided into four main stages:

  • Computer-Based Test (CBT): The first stage is a CBT to assess the candidates’ knowledge of various subjects.
  • Physical Efficiency Test (PET): Candidates who are shortlisted from the CBT will have to pass the PET to prove their physical fitness.
  • Document Verification (DV): Successful candidates from the PET will then proceed to have their educational and personal documents verified.
  • Medical Examination: The final stage involves a medical fitness test to ensure candidates are fit for their duties in the railways.

Question: What should candidates do to prepare for the upcoming exam?

Answer: It is recommended that all aspirants keep a close watch on their regional RRB websites for the latest announcements. They should also make travel and logistical arrangements after receiving their city intimation slip and ensure they carry a valid photo ID along with their admit card on the day of the exam.
