North Western Railway Apprentice: Skill Development Opportunity Under Apprentices Act 1961

Transforming 2162 ITI-Qualified Youth into Skilled Railway Technicians Across Rajasthan

The RRC NWR Apprentice Recruitment 2025 offers 2,162 training slots across four divisions (Ajmer, Bikaner, Jaipur, Jodhpur) under Apprentices Act 1961. This merit-based engagement provides hands-on industrial training to ITI-certified candidates in various technical trades preparing them for regular railway employment.

Division-Wise Distribution:

  • DRM Office Ajmer: 426 posts (Fitter, Electrician, Welder, Carpenter, Machinist trades)
  • DRM Office Bikaner: 475 posts (multiple technical trades across workshops)
  • DRM Office Jaipur: 545 posts (largest allocation with diverse trade opportunities)
  • DRM Office Jodhpur: 450 posts (technical training at workshops/units)
  • Workshop-specific: BTC Ajmer (68), BTC Loco Ajmer (68), Carriage Workshops (33-68 posts)

Popular Trades Available:

  • Fitter: Mechanical fitting, machine operation, equipment maintenance
  • Electrician: Electrical installations, wiring, motor maintenance
  • Welder (Gas & Arc): Metal joining, fabrication work
  • Machinist: Lathe operation, precision machining
  • Carpenter: Woodwork, furniture, coach interiors
  • Painter: Coach painting, surface preparation, finishing
  • Diesel Mechanic: Engine maintenance, locomotive repairs

Stipend Structure:

  • 1st year: ₹7,000/month (basic training allowance)
  • 2nd year: ₹8,050/month (15% increment after satisfactory performance)
  • 3rd year: ₹9,000/month (for 3-year trades only)
  • Annual increment: Based on performance evaluation and attendance

Apprentices receive comprehensive training combining theoretical knowledge with practical workshop experience ensuring job-ready skills for railway maintenance operations and potential absorption as regular railway employees post-training completion.

Merit-Based Selection: Simple Average of 10th and ITI Marks

No Written Exam or Interview—Pure Academic Merit Decides Selection

The RRC NWR Apprentice selection 2025 follows transparent merit-based process without written examination or personal interview. Merit list prepared by calculating simple average of Class 10th percentage and ITI marks percentage ensuring objective selection purely on academic credentials.

Merit Calculation Formula:

  • 10th percentage: All subjects marks considered (not selective subjects)
  • ITI percentage: As per provisional/final certificate issued by NCVT/SCVT
  • Merit = (10th % + ITI %) / 2
  • Example: 10th (65%) + ITI (75%) = 140/2 = 70% merit score

Tie-Breaking Rules:

  • First preference: Candidate with older age (date of birth considered)
  • Second preference: Candidate who passed Class 10th examination earlier
  • Third preference: Alphabetical order of candidate name

Application Process:

  • Registration: Online only at rrcactapp.in (03 Oct - 02 Nov 2025)
  • Application fee: ₹100 (General/OBC/EWS), Free for SC/ST/PH/Women
  • Documents upload: Scanned photo, signature, 10th marksheet, ITI certificate
  • Payment: Online via debit/credit card, net banking, UPI
  • Confirmation: Download application receipt after successful submission

Post-Merit List Process:

  • Merit list publication: Expected December 2025 on official website
  • Document verification: At respective DRM offices/workshops (candidates called as per merit)
  • Medical examination: Railway medical standards (vision, hearing, physical fitness)
  • Engagement letter: Issued after successful DV and medical clearance
  • Training commencement: January-February 2026 at allotted units

Candidates advised to keep 10th marksheet and ITI certificate handy during application. Any discrepancy in uploaded documents leads to rejection at document verification stage regardless of merit position.

Apprentice Training Benefits and Post-Training Employment Prospects

₹7,000-₹9,000 Monthly Stipend Plus 20% Reservation in Railway Recruitment

The NWR Apprentice training program offers comprehensive skill development with monthly stipend, free training facilities, and significant advantage in future railway recruitments. Apprentices completing training get 20% reservation quota in subsequent RRB/RRC recruitments for Group C technical posts.

Training Duration:

  • 1-year trades: Fitter, Electrician, Welder, Carpenter (12 months intensive training)
  • 2-year trades: Advanced machinist, tool & die maker (24 months specialized training)
  • 3-year trades: Specific specialized trades as per NCVT norms (36 months)

Training Facilities Provided:

  • Hands-on workshop training: Latest machinery, tools, equipment at railway workshops
  • Theoretical classes: Technical knowledge, safety procedures, railway-specific protocols
  • On-job training: Real-time exposure to locomotive/coach maintenance operations
  • Skill assessment: Periodic evaluation by qualified railway supervisors/instructors
  • Certification: National Apprenticeship Certificate (NAC) on successful completion

Post-Training Employment Advantages:

  • 20% quota: Reserved for apprentices in RRB Group C (Technician/Helper) recruitments
  • Higher preference: Ex-apprentices given priority in railway technical posts
  • Experience credit: Training period counts toward seniority after regular appointment
  • Direct absorption: Outstanding performers may get direct employment offers (case-by-case basis)

Additional Benefits:

  • ESI coverage: Medical insurance under Employee State Insurance scheme
  • EPF contribution: Provident fund benefits (if applicable as per rules)
  • Accident insurance: Coverage during training period for workplace injuries
  • Leave entitlement: 12 days annual leave + festival holidays
  • Railway ID card: Access to railway colonies, facilities during training

Apprentices maintaining 80%+ attendance and good performance ratings receive appreciation certificates boosting their profiles for future railway/PSU job applications. Training at prestigious railway workshops like Ajmer BTC, Bikaner Carriage Workshop adds significant value to resume.

North Western Railway: Connecting Rajasthan's Heritage with Modern Rail Network

Operating 5,000+ Route Km Across 4 Divisions Serving 10 Crore+ Passengers Annually

The North Western Railway (NWR) headquartered at Jaipur operates extensive network across Rajasthan connecting historic cities, pilgrimage sites, and industrial hubs. Formed in 2002, NWR manages 4 divisions with 5,459 route kilometers serving both passenger and freight traffic in India's largest state.

NWR Infrastructure:

  • 4 divisions: Jaipur, Ajmer, Bikaner, Jodhpur (each with DRM office, workshops, units)
  • 400+ railway stations: Major terminals, junction stations, small rural stations
  • 5,459 route km: Connecting Delhi-Mumbai, Delhi-Ahmedabad corridors
  • 50,000+ employees: Locomotive pilots, technicians, operating staff, administrative personnel
  • Major workshops: Ajmer BTC (coaching), Bikaner/Jodhpur carriage workshops, loco sheds

Key Railway Stations:

  • Jaipur Junction: State capital connectivity, luxury tourist trains (Palace on Wheels)
  • Ajmer Junction: Pilgrimage hub (Pushkar), major locomotive shed
  • Jodhpur Junction: Blue City gateway, defense movement corridor
  • Bikaner Junction: Desert region connectivity, cross-border strategic importance
  • Abu Road: Mount Abu hill station access, Western Railway junction

Operational Achievements (2024):

  • Passenger traffic: 10.5 crore passengers carried annually
  • Freight traffic: 45 million tonnes (cement, minerals, petroleum products)
  • Punctuality: 85% trains running on time (above national average)
  • Electrification: 65% network electrified (target 100% by 2027)
  • Safety record: Zero passenger fatalities in 2023-24 (excellent safety performance)

Apprentice Role in NWR Operations:

  • Locomotive maintenance: Assisting in diesel/electric loco repairs, inspection schedules
  • Coach maintenance: POH (Periodic Overhauling) of passenger coaches, AC/non-AC maintenance
  • Track maintenance: Supporting permanent way staff in rail/sleeper replacement
  • Electrical systems: Overhead equipment maintenance, signaling system support
  • Workshop operations: Fabrication, welding, fitting jobs at major workshops

Recent Projects:

  • Gauge conversion: Meter gauge to broad gauge (Marwar-Merta City section completed)
  • New lines: Rewari-Ringus (98 km), Phulera-Kuchaman City doubling
  • Station redevelopment: Jaipur, Ajmer stations under Amrit Bharat scheme
  • Vande Bharat: Semi-high-speed train services on Jaipur-Delhi, Ajmer-Delhi routes

Career Growth Path:

  • Apprentice → Helper/Assistant (Group D via RRB recruitment with 20% quota)
  • Helper → Technician Grade-III (Group C promotion after 5-7 years)
  • Technician-III → Technician-II → Technician-I (merit-based promotions)
  • Senior Technician → Junior Engineer (ALP/Technician) (via competitive exams)
  • Top positions: Section Engineer, Divisional Mechanical Engineer (20-25 years career)

NWR apprentices contribute to maintaining India's lifeline—the Indian Railways—serving millions daily. Training at NWR workshops provides foundational skills applicable across manufacturing, automobile, and heavy engineering sectors beyond railways.
