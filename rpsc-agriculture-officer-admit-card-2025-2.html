Preparation Tips for RPSC Agriculture Officer (AO/ARO/AARO)

Focused Preparation for Specialized Agriculture Post Exams

The RPSC Agriculture Officer Recruitment 2024 covers highly specialized roles like Agronomy, Entomology, Plant Pathology, and Chemistry. Your preparation must be laser-focused on your specific discipline, covering both theoretical concepts and their practical applications relevant to agricultural practices in Rajasthan. Alongside the subject knowledge, prepare thoroughly for the General Knowledge section pertaining to Rajasthan's history, geography, and current affairs. Practice previous years' papers, and mock tests, and revise important formulas and terminologies regularly. Since the exam is very near, prioritize high-yield topics and previous revision notes.

Post-Specific Eligibility and Age Criteria for RPSC Recruitment

Detailed Overview of RPSC Agriculture Vacancy Eligibility

This RPSC notification is significant due to the variety of specialized posts, totaling 241 vacancies, ranging from Assistant Agriculture Officer (AAO) to Agriculture Research Officer (ARO). Eligibility varies; AAO (NSA) requires a Bachelor's degree, while all ARO and Statistical Officer posts require a Second Class Master's degree in a highly specific subject area (e.g., M.Sc. Agri. with Agronomy). Age limits also differ, starting from 18 to 20 years minimum age post-wise, up to a maximum of 40 years. Candidates must match their Application ID with the exact post they applied for when downloading the Admit Card to confirm the correct exam schedule and syllabus.
