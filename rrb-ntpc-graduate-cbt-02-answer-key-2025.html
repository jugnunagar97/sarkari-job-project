How To Check & Download RRB NTPC CBT-2 Answer Key 2025
  • Visit official website of your respective Railway Recruitment Board (RRB) zone from regional links.
  • On homepage, go to "Latest Notifications" or "Recruitment" section prominently displayed at top.
  • Find and click on link titled "RRB NTPC Graduate Level CBT-2 Answer Key 2025" or "CEN 05/2024".
  • You'll be redirected to centralized login page at rrb.digialm.com for credential verification.
  • Enter your Registration Number in first field provided on login screen for identification.
  • Enter Password or Date of Birth in second field for security authentication purposes.
  • Fill verification code or captcha displayed on screen correctly to proceed with answer key access.
  • Once logged in successfully, your Answer Key and Response Sheet will be displayed on screen.
  • Review question paper, your responses, and official correct answers provided for all 100 questions.
  • Download and save Answer Key PDF for future reference before objection deadline expires.
  • Carefully match your answers to calculate estimated score considering negative marking of 0.33 marks.
  • If you find discrepancies or errors, raise objections online within given time period till 23 October 2025.
  • Pay objection fee of ₹50 per question through online payment gateway (refundable if objection valid).

NTPC Graduate: Indian Railways' Supervisory Staff Backbone

Filling 8,113 Graduate Level Positions Across Indian Railways Managing Operations Nationwide

The RRB NTPC Graduate Recruitment CEN 05/2024 fills 8,113 critical supervisory positions across Indian Railways' Non-Technical Popular Categories. These graduate-level posts including Station Master, Goods Train Manager, Commercial cum Ticket Supervisor, Senior Clerk cum Typist, and Junior Accounts Assistant cum Typist manage railway operations, commercial activities, goods transportation, accounts management, and administrative functions ensuring smooth functioning of world's fourth-largest railway network operating 12,000+ trains daily carrying 23 million passengers serving India's 140 crore+ population across 68,000+ route kilometers. NTPC officers supervise station operations, train movements, ticketing, commercial transactions, accounting, revenue collection, and administrative coordination under Railway Board supervision contributing efficient passenger services, freight operations, and organizational excellence.

Post-Wise Job Responsibilities:

  • Station Master (994 posts): Managing station operations, train movements, passenger safety, signaling, platform management, staff supervision
  • Goods Train Manager (3,144 posts): Supervising goods train operations, loading/unloading, freight documentation, safety compliance, revenue collection
  • Commercial cum Ticket Supervisor (1,736 posts): Managing ticketing operations, reservation systems, commercial activities, revenue accounting, customer service
  • Junior Accounts Assistant cum Typist (1,507 posts): Accounting operations, financial records, budget management, bill processing, audit compliance, typing correspondence
  • Senior Clerk cum Typist (732 posts): Administrative documentation, correspondence, record keeping, typing official documents, office management

Salary and Benefits:

  • Pay Level 6: ₹35,400-₹1,12,400/month (7th CPC) for Station Master, Goods Train Manager, Commercial Supervisor
  • Pay Level 5: ₹29,200-₹92,300/month for Junior Accounts Assistant, Senior Clerk positions
  • Gross in-hand: ₹50,000-₹60,000/month initially (basic + DA + HRA + TA + other allowances)
  • Annual CTC: ₹7-9 lakh initially including all components and perquisites

Indian Railways employees enjoy comprehensive central government benefits including DA (50%), HRA, transport allowance, medical facilities, GPF, pension, railway passes (free travel), LTC ensuring rewarding railway careers.

CBT-II Answer Key Analysis: Exam Pattern and Expected Cutoff

Answer Key Released 17 October for 1,21,931 Candidates Exam Held 13 October 2025

The RRB NTPC CBT-II Answer Key 2025 enables 1,21,931 shortlisted candidates calculating expected scores before result declaration. Released on 17 October 2025 for examination conducted 13 October, the provisional answer key allows candidates verifying responses, identifying discrepancies, and raising objections till 23 October 2025 by paying ₹50 per question (refundable if objection accepted). The examination witnessed candidates from across India competing for 8,113 prestigious graduate-level railway positions demonstrating intense competition. Final answer key will be released after objection review by expert committee followed by CBT-II result expected November 2025 announcing selected candidates for aptitude test, typing test, document verification stages.

CBT-II Exam Pattern Structure:

  • General Awareness (50 questions): Current affairs, Indian economy, geography, history, polity, sports, science, railways
  • Mathematics (35 questions): Number system, decimals, fractions, LCM/HCF, ratio, percentage, time-work, profit-loss, geometry, trigonometry, data interpretation
  • General Intelligence & Reasoning (30 questions): Analogies, series, coding-decoding, relationships, directions, syllogisms, puzzles, classification
  • General Science (25 questions): Physics, chemistry, biology fundamentals covering 10th standard CBSE/ICSE syllabus
  • Total questions: 140 MCQs across four sections (only 120 best questions counted for scoring)
  • Total marks: 120 marks (1 mark per question, only top 120 answers considered)
  • Duration: 90 minutes (1 hour 30 minutes) for completing examination
  • Negative marking: 1/3rd (0.33) marks deducted for each incorrect answer
  • Exam mode: Computer-based test conducted by NTA at centers across India
  • Normalization: Applied across shifts for fair evaluation due to multiple exam sessions

Marks Calculation Method:

  • Formula: Total Score = (Correct Answers × 1) - (Wrong Answers × 0.33)
  • Best 120: Only top 120 scores from 140 questions counted for final marks
  • Example: 95 correct + 30 wrong + 15 unattempted from 140 questions
  • Calculation: (95 × 1) - (30 × 0.33) = 95 - 10 = 85 marks (considering best 120 answers)
  • Percentage: (Total Score / 120) × 100 = Percentage achieved
  • Normalization: Raw scores normalized using statistical formula considering difficulty level across shifts

Expected Cutoff Marks (Normalized - Category-Wise):

  • UR (Unreserved/General): 75-85/120 (62-70% marks) due to high competition for supervisory positions
  • EWS (Economically Weaker Section): 70-80/120 (58-67% marks) slight relaxation from general cutoff
  • OBC-NCL (Non-Creamy Layer): 65-75/120 (54-62% marks) moderate relaxation for backward classes
  • SC (Scheduled Caste): 55-65/120 (46-54% marks) substantial relaxation ensuring representation
  • ST (Scheduled Tribe): 50-60/120 (42-50% marks) highest relaxation for tribal candidates
  • ExSM (Ex-Servicemen): 70-80/120 (58-67% marks) for defense background candidates with experience
  • PwD (Persons with Disabilities): 45-55/120 (37-46% marks) special consideration for disabled candidates
  • Post-wise variation: Station Master, Commercial Supervisor may have higher cutoffs than clerical positions
  • Zone-wise variation: Cutoffs vary across RRB zones depending on vacancies, candidates performance

Candidates scoring above expected cutoffs should prepare for next selection stages including aptitude test (for Station Master, Goods Train Manager), typing test (for clerical posts), document verification, and medical examination ensuring final selection.

Career Progression: NTPC Officer to Group A Gazetted Officer

₹50,000 Starting Salary with Promotions to Senior Management Positions

The Indian Railways NTPC career offers excellent progression opportunities advancing from supervisory level to senior management positions. Officers gain valuable railway operations experience, develop specialized skills in commercial operations, station management, goods transportation, accounting, and qualify for promotions to Assistant Station Master, Chief Commercial Inspector, Senior Accounts Officer, Section Controller levels managing divisions, coordinating multiple stations, supervising large teams eventually reaching Group A gazetted officer positions through competitive exams or departmental promotions contributing Indian Railways' operational excellence serving nation's transportation needs ensuring efficient passenger services, freight operations, and organizational growth supporting India's economic development through robust railway network connectivity.

Career Advancement Path:

  • Entry Level NTPC (0-5 years): Station Master, Goods Train Manager, Commercial Supervisor managing operations, staff, revenue
  • Senior Supervisor (5-10 years): Assistant Station Master, Chief Commercial Inspector handling larger stations, complex operations
  • Section Controller (10-15 years): Managing railway sections, coordinating multiple stations, train operations, commercial activities
  • Divisional Supervisor (15-20 years): Divisional commercial inspector, senior accounts officer managing division-level operations
  • Group A Officer (20+ years): Through LDCE/GDCE exams reaching officer positions managing zonal operations, policy formulation

Job Responsibilities and Work Profile:

  • Station operations: Managing train arrivals/departures, platform allocation, passenger announcements, safety protocols, staff supervision
  • Commercial activities: Ticketing operations, reservation management, revenue collection, commercial contracts, passenger amenities
  • Goods management: Supervising loading/unloading operations, freight documentation, safety compliance, revenue accounting, customer dealing
  • Accounting duties: Maintaining accounts, budget preparation, bill processing, audit compliance, financial reporting, revenue reconciliation
  • Administrative work: Staff management, correspondence, record keeping, report preparation, coordination with departments
  • Safety responsibilities: Ensuring passenger safety, train operations safety, emergency response, accident prevention, regulatory compliance

Comprehensive Benefits Package:

  • Basic salary: ₹35,400-₹1,12,400/month (Pay Level 6) with regular annual increments based on performance
  • Dearness Allowance: Currently 50% of basic (revised biannually) compensating inflation impact nationwide
  • House Rent Allowance: 8-24% of basic depending on city classification (X/Y/Z categories)
  • Transport Allowance: ₹3,600-₹7,200/month for commuting expenses based on city classification
  • Running Allowance: Additional allowance for operational staff working irregular hours, shift duties
  • Railway Passes: Free railway travel for self, family, dependent parents across India (AC/Non-AC classes)
  • Medical facilities: Comprehensive railway hospital treatment + reimbursement for self, family members
  • GPF accumulation: General Provident Fund with interest building substantial retirement corpus tax-free
  • Pension: Monthly pension after retirement (central government pension scheme) ensuring post-retirement security
  • Gratuity: Lump sum payment on retirement (15 days salary per completed year of service)
  • LTC: Leave Travel Concession covering family travel expenses once every two years
  • Railway quarters: Subsidized railway accommodation at nominal rent or HRA for private accommodation
  • Children education: Education allowance for children's schooling, scholarship schemes, concessional admissions

Work Environment and Culture:

  • Working hours: 8-hour shifts with rotational duties (morning/evening/night shifts) for operational posts
  • Posting locations: Railway stations, divisions, zones across India (preference considered during allocation)
  • Transfer policy: Transfers every 3-5 years across divisions ensuring diverse experience, skill development
  • Professional environment: Disciplined railway culture requiring punctuality, safety consciousness, teamwork, dedication
  • Job security: Permanent central government employment with pension ensuring lifetime career stability

Indian Railways NTPC positions provide prestigious careers combining supervisory authority, excellent compensation, comprehensive benefits, promotion opportunities to senior management making railway service attractive profession for graduates.

Indian Railways: World's Fourth-Largest Railway Network Since 1853

Operating 12,000+ Trains Daily Across 68,000 km Network Serving 23 Million Passengers

The Indian Railways established 1853 serves as lifeline of India operating world's fourth-largest railway network spanning 68,000+ route kilometers across diverse terrain from Himalayas to coastal regions. With 17 railway zones, 68 divisions, 7,000+ stations, the network operates 12,000+ trains daily including 7,000 passenger trains and 5,000 freight trains carrying 23 million passengers and 3 million tons freight daily generating ₹2 lakh crore+ annual revenue employing 12 lakh+ personnel making it world's eighth-largest employer. Railway Recruitment Boards conduct centralized recruitment ensuring merit-based transparent selection of competent staff maintaining operational excellence, safety standards, customer service quality supporting India's economic growth through efficient transportation connectivity enabling trade, tourism, employment generation under Ministry of Railways contributing nation's development vision.

Major Railway Operations:

  • Passenger services: Rajdhani, Shatabdi, Duronto express trains, mail/express, passenger trains, suburban locals, metros
