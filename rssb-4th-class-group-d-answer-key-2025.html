Group D: Rajasthan's Support Staff Backbone

Filling 53,749 Class IV Positions Across Rajasthan Government Departments

The RSSB 4th Class Recruitment 2024 fills 53,749 critical Group D positions across various Rajasthan Government departments and subordinate offices. Also known as Class IV employees, these support staff handle essential duties including peon services, office assistance, cleaning, maintenance, security, transportation, and other support functions ensuring smooth government operations serving Rajasthan's 8 crore+ population across 33 districts. Group D employees work in offices, schools, hospitals, courts, police stations, and other government establishments providing crucial support services enabling efficient functioning of administrative machinery under Administrative Reforms Department managing state's largest recruitment drive for support staff positions contributing employment generation and administrative efficiency.

Department-Wise Recruitment:

  • Education Department: Peons, helpers, cleaners, gardeners for government schools, colleges, educational institutions across state
  • Health Department: Ward attendants, hospital helpers, sanitation staff, drivers for government hospitals, medical colleges
  • Police Department: Constables (non-combatant), drivers, office helpers supporting police administration, law enforcement
  • Judicial Department: Court peons, process servers, cleaners, drivers for district courts, High Court offices
  • Administrative Offices: Peons, drivers, gardeners, security staff for collectorate, secretariat, government offices
  • PWD/Infrastructure: Helpers, sweepers, gardeners, maintenance staff for Public Works Department establishments

Salary and Benefits:

  • Pay Level 1: ₹18,000-₹56,900 per month (7th Pay Commission) for 4th Class employees
  • Gross in-hand: ₹20,000-₹25,000/month initially (basic + DA + HRA + other allowances)
  • Annual CTC: ₹3-3.5 lakh initially including all components and perquisites

Rajasthan Government Group D employees enjoy comprehensive state benefits including DA (50%), HRA, medical facilities, GPF, pension, LTC, uniform allowance, promotion opportunities to higher grades ensuring stable employment.

Answer Key Analysis: Exam Pattern and Expected Cutoff

Answer Key Released 17 October for 21 Lakh Candidates Across Six Shifts

The RSSB Group D Answer Key 2025 enables over 21 lakh candidates calculating expected scores before result declaration. Released on 17 October 2025 for examinations conducted 19-21 September across six shifts (morning and evening for three days), the answer key provides correct answers for all question sets helping candidates verify their responses. The examination witnessed massive participation demonstrating high unemployment levels and demand for government jobs in Rajasthan. Objection window will open after Diwali allowing candidates challenging answers with valid proof ensuring transparent evaluation process before final result preparation expected December 2025.

Exam Pattern Structure:

  • Rajasthan GK (40 questions): History, geography, culture, economy, polity, current affairs of Rajasthan state
  • General Knowledge (20 questions): Indian history, geography, polity, economy, science, current affairs, important events
  • Reasoning Ability (20 questions): Logical reasoning, analogies, series, coding-decoding, puzzles, directions, blood relations
  • Mathematics (20 questions): Arithmetic, percentage, ratio-proportion, profit-loss, time-work, simple/compound interest, data interpretation
  • General Science (20 questions): Physics, chemistry, biology basics, environmental studies, scientific inventions, human body
  • Total questions: 120 Multiple Choice Questions across five sections
  • Total marks: 100 marks (weighted marking system with different sections having different weightage)
  • Duration: 2 hours (120 minutes) for completing examination
  • Negative marking: 0.33 marks (1/3rd mark) deducted for each wrong answer
  • Exam mode: Computer-based test conducted at centers across Rajasthan cities

Marks Calculation Method:

  • Formula: Total Score = (Correct Answers × Marks) - (Wrong Answers × 0.33)
  • Example: 85 correct + 25 wrong + 10 unattempted from 120 questions
  • Calculation: Assuming average 0.83 marks per question = (85 × 0.83) - (25 × 0.33) = 70.55 - 8.25 = 62.30 marks
  • Percentage: (Total Score / 100) × 100 = Percentage achieved
  • Category-wise merit: Separate cutoffs for General, OBC, SC, ST, EWS categories

Expected Cutoff Marks (Category-Wise):

  • General/UR category: 55-65/100 (55-65% marks) due to massive competition for limited vacancies
  • EWS category: 50-60/100 (50-60% marks) slight relaxation from general cutoff
  • OBC (Non-Creamy Layer): 48-58/100 (48-58% marks) moderate relaxation for backward classes
  • SC category: 42-52/100 (42-52% marks) substantial relaxation ensuring adequate representation
  • ST category: 38-48/100 (38-48% marks) highest relaxation for tribal candidates
  • PwD category: 35-45/100 (35-45% marks) special consideration for persons with disabilities
  • Female candidates: May receive additional relaxation as per Rajasthan Government reservation policy
  • Cutoff varies: Final cutoffs depend on difficulty level, number of candidates, vacancies distribution

Candidates scoring above expected cutoffs should prepare for document verification stage bringing original certificates ensuring final selection to prestigious Rajasthan Government Group D positions.

Career Progression: Group D to Senior Assistant

₹20,000 Starting Salary with Promotions to Higher Grade Positions

The Rajasthan Group D career offers structured progression advancing from entry-level support staff to senior assistant positions. Employees gain valuable government service experience, develop workplace skills, understand administrative procedures, and qualify for promotions to Group C clerical positions through departmental examinations or direct promotions based on seniority ensuring career growth opportunities. Long service Group D employees often promoted to higher positions managing small teams, handling responsible duties contributing department operations serving state government ensuring lifetime employment security with comprehensive benefits supporting families through stable income.

Career Advancement Path:

  • Class IV Employee (0-10 years): Entry level peon, helper, cleaner duties under supervisor guidance
  • Senior Class IV (10-15 years): Supervisory role guiding junior staff, handling responsible assignments
  • Class III (15-20 years): Promotion to clerical positions through exams or seniority managing records
  • Senior Assistant (20-25 years): Administrative support, complex documentation, junior staff supervision
  • Head Clerk (25-30 years): Section management, multiple clerks supervision, departmental coordination

Job Responsibilities and Work Profile:

  • Office assistance: File carrying, document delivery, copying, photocopying, maintaining office supplies, tea/water service
  • Cleaning duties: Office cleaning, sweeping, dusting, maintaining cleanliness in government premises ensuring hygiene
  • Security support: Guarding office premises, checking entries, managing visitors, ensuring safety of government property
  • Transportation: Driving official vehicles, vehicle maintenance, transportation of officers, documents, materials
  • Maintenance work: Minor repairs, gardening, plumbing assistance, electrical work support, building maintenance
  • Public dealing: Assisting visitors, providing directions, basic information, maintaining decorum in office premises

Comprehensive Benefits Package:

  • Basic salary: ₹18,000-₹56,900/month (Pay Level 1) with regular annual increments
  • Dearness Allowance: Currently 50% of basic (revised biannually based on inflation index)
  • House Rent Allowance: 8-24% of basic depending on posting location (Metro/Urban/Rural classification)
  • Uniform allowance: Annual grant for purchasing uniforms for peon, driver, other uniformed positions
  • Medical facilities: Government hospital treatment + reimbursement for self and dependent family members
  • GPF accumulation: General Provident Fund with interest building retirement corpus tax-free
  • Pension: Monthly pension after retirement ensuring post-retirement financial security for old age
  • Gratuity: Lump sum payment on retirement (15 days salary for each completed year)
  • LTC: Leave Travel Concession covering family travel expenses once every two years
  • Festival advances: Interest-free advances during festivals for meeting personal expenses

Work Environment and Culture:

  • Working hours: 9:30 AM - 5:30 PM typically (Monday-Friday) with Saturday half-day in some departments
  • Posting locations: Government offices, schools, hospitals, courts, police stations across 33 districts of Rajasthan
  • Transfer policy: Limited transfers within district/division ensuring family stability, local community connections
  • Physical demands: Standing duties, walking, carrying files, cleaning requiring physical fitness, stamina
  • Job security: Permanent government employment with pension ensuring lifetime financial security for families

Rajasthan Group D positions provide stable careers for 10th pass candidates offering government employment, regular income, comprehensive benefits, promotion opportunities making support staff attractive profession.
