RSSB NHM & RajMES Result 2025 Out

How To Check & Download RSSB NHM & RajMES Result 2025
  • Visit the Official RSSB Website at https://rssb.rajasthan.gov.in/.
  • On the homepage, click on the "Results" or "Candidate Corner" tab.
  • Find the link titled "NHM & RajMES Result 2025" or "NHM / RajMES Recruitment Examination Result 2025."
  • Click on the Result Link – The result will open in a PDF format displaying the list of shortlisted candidates.
  • Search Your Roll Number / Name – Press Ctrl + F and type your Roll Number or Name to quickly find your result.
  • Check Qualification Status – If your Roll Number appears in the list, you have qualified for the next stage of the selection process.
  • Download the Result PDF – Click on the download icon or use Ctrl + S to save the result PDF to your device.
  • Print the Result – Take a hard copy of the result for future reference, especially for document verification.

Why RSSB NHM & RajMES Result 2025 Matters for Healthcare Professionals

Why RSSB NHM & RajMES Result 2025 Represents Major Career Opportunity

The RSSB NHM Result 2025, declared on 17 October 2025, marks a pivotal moment for healthcare professionals across Rajasthan. This result covers 13,398 contractual positions under National Health Mission and Rajasthan Medical Education Society, representing one of the state's largest healthcare recruitment drives. With 8,256 NHM positions focusing on grassroots healthcare delivery and 5,142 RajMES positions strengthening medical education infrastructure, this recruitment opens diverse career pathways for nursing graduates, paramedical professionals, and technical specialists. The examination conducted between 02-13 June 2025 attracted over 4.2 lakh applicants, creating a competitive ratio of 31:1. Qualified candidates will serve across Rajasthan's 33 districts, particularly in rural areas where the doctor-to-patient ratio stands at 1:1890. These contractual positions offer competitive salaries ranging from ₹15,000 to ₹40,000 monthly with comprehensive benefits including ESI coverage and maternity leave. Successfully selected candidates join a network of 62,000 government healthcare workers, gaining access to continuous professional development programs and future permanent employment opportunities through regularization drives.

Document Verification Process for RSSB NHM & RajMES 2025

Complete Guide to Document Verification and Next Steps After Result

After the RSSB result declaration on 17 October 2025, qualified candidates must prepare for document verification scheduled to commence in early November 2025. The verification process occurs at district-level centers across all 33 districts of Rajasthan, with RSMSSB processing 200-300 candidates daily per center. Candidates must present original educational certificates, medical registration certificates for clinical positions, caste certificates if applicable, domicile proof, and valid identity documents. The board conducts stringent verification including online certificate authentication with issuing universities and medical councils. Historical data shows approximately 78 percent of candidates successfully complete document verification, with 22 percent disqualified due to certificate discrepancies or incomplete documentation. Following document verification, clinical positions require medical fitness examinations including vision tests and general physical assessments. Candidates receive appointment letters within 60-75 days of result publication, specifying posting locations and training schedules. Mandatory orientation training ranging from 15-45 days occurs at state training institutes before official duty commencement, typically by January 2026. Check rssb.rajasthan.gov.in regularly for detailed schedules and document checklists.

Career Prospects and Salary Benefits in NHM & RajMES Positions

Salary Structure, Growth Opportunities, and Long-Term Career Benefits

The RSSB NHM positions 2025 offer competitive remuneration with Community Health Officers earning ₹32,000-₹38,000 monthly, Nurse Grade-II receiving ₹28,000-₹32,000, and Medical Lab Technicians getting ₹22,000-₹26,000 depending on qualifications. Unlike private sector roles, these government contractual positions provide structured annual increments of 7-10 percent based on performance evaluations. High performers can receive merit increments of 12-15 percent and advance to supervisory roles within three years, carrying 25-35 percent salary increases. The positions include comprehensive benefits: ESI health coverage up to ₹5 lakh annually, accident insurance of ₹10 lakh, 180 days paid maternity leave, and housing allowances for rural postings. Career progression includes potential permanent cadre absorption through regularization drives—approximately 3,200 contractual staff were absorbed into permanent positions during 2021-2023. Contractual service experience counts as relevant work experience in subsequent permanent recruitment examinations, with some positions offering age relaxation benefits. These roles serve as legitimate pathways to permanent government employment while providing financial stability, professional development opportunities, and meaningful contributions to public healthcare delivery across Rajasthan's underserved communities.

About RSMSSB Organization and NHM Infrastructure in Rajasthan

Understanding RSMSSB's Role and Healthcare Infrastructure Framework

The Rajasthan Subordinate and Ministerial Services Selection Board (RSMSSB), established under state legislation, conducts 12-15 major recruitment examinations annually, filling 25,000-35,000 government positions across departments. The board's technological infrastructure supports computer-based testing and OMR examinations through 847 centers, ensuring accessibility for rural and urban candidates. RSMSSB's transparent computerized result compilation eliminates human intervention, earning reputation for fairness with less than 0.5 percent results challenged successfully in courts. Rajasthan's National Health Mission operates 2,185 Primary Health Centers, 573 Community Health Centers, and 35 District Hospitals serving 5.12 crore population. Despite this extensive network, 28 percent nursing positions and 22 percent paramedical positions remain vacant, necessitating current recruitment. The state's 2025-26 health budget stands at ₹14,200 crore with ₹3,800 crore allocated specifically for NHM programs and contractual staff salaries. Under the 2024-2029 Health Infrastructure Development Plan, Rajasthan aims to establish 200 additional PHCs and upgrade 100 CHCs, creating 18,000-22,000 additional healthcare positions by 2028, ensuring recurring opportunities for healthcare professionals seeking government employment.
