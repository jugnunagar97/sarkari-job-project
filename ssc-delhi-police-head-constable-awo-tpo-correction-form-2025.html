How to Make Corrections in SSC Delhi Police Head Constable Application 2025
  • Visit the official Staff Selection Commission website at https://ssc.gov.in/login.
  • Click on "Login" option on the homepage to access the candidate portal and secure area.
  • Enter your Registration Number/User ID and Password that you used during original application submission and registration.
  • After successful authentication, navigate to the "Active Applications" or "Submitted Applications" section in your dashboard.
  • Locate the application for "Delhi Police Head Constable (AWO/TPO) Recruitment 2025" from your submitted applications list displayed on screen.
  • Click on the "Edit Application" or "Make Corrections" button available against the application entry to begin editing process.
  • The correction form will open showing all sections of your submitted application form with previously entered information pre-filled.
  • Carefully review each section including personal details, educational qualifications, category information, and uploaded documents for any errors.
  • Make necessary corrections in editable fields such as name, father's name, address, category claims, qualification details, and examination center preferences.
  • If required, re-upload documents including photographs, signatures, or certificates if there were errors in previously uploaded files ensuring they meet specifications.
  • Review all corrections thoroughly against source documents before final submission as multiple corrections may not be allowed.
  • Click on "Submit" button to save your corrections permanently in the SSC database after final verification.
  • Take printout of the corrected application form for your future reference and maintaining personal records throughout recruitment process.
  • Note that correction facility is available only from 31 October to 02 November 2025 (3-day window); no corrections accepted after deadline.

SSC Delhi Police Head Constable Recruitment 2025: Mode of Selection
  • Computer Based Test (CBT): Objective-type examination covering General Intelligence & Reasoning, General Awareness, Numerical Aptitude, and Elementary Mathematics relevant to constable-level duties
  • Physical Efficiency Test (PET): Age-wise standards including running test (1600-1200 meters), long jump, and high jump with multiple attempts allowed for qualification
  • Measurement Test (MT): Verification of height and physical measurements as per prescribed standards for male and female police personnel
  • Trade Test: Specialized skill assessment for specific constabulary trades such as driver, clerk, or specialized police functions based on requirement
  • Document Verification: Comprehensive verification of 10+2 certificates with Science and Mathematics, category certificates, age proof, and other eligibility documents
  • Medical Examination: Complete medical fitness assessment by authorized Delhi Police medical boards ensuring candidates meet stringent health standards for operational policing duties

Delhi Police Head Constable Position Benefits, Career Path, and Job Profile

Comprehensive Compensation Package and Career Opportunities in Delhi Police Constabulary

Delhi Police Head Constables selected through SSC recruitment and assigned as AWO (Armed Women Orderly) or TPO (Trainee Police Officer) receive attractive compensation packages aligned with 7th Central Pay Commission recommendations for Group-C police personnel. Head Constables begin at pay level-4 with basic pay of ₹25,500 per month. Including Dearness Allowance at approximately 50%, Police Duty Allowance (Special Allowance for operational duties), Transport Allowance (₹3,600-₹7,200), House Rent Allowance at 27% for Delhi (X-class city classification), and specialized operation allowances, the total monthly gross salary ranges from ₹45,000-₹55,000 for newly appointed Head Constables. This compensation structure ensures decent living standards in Delhi's expensive market while providing financial security for young professionals entering specialized law enforcement roles handling armed duties or operational policing tasks.

Comprehensive Police Service Benefits:

  • Permanent Government Appointment: Lifetime employment as government of India servant with Delhi Police offering security till superannuation at age 60 years without performance anxiety
  • Police Welfare Corpus: Special police welfare funds providing financial assistance for housing loans, vehicle purchases, children's education, emergency medical expenses, and family hardship situations
  • Specialized Training Programs: Regular capacity building on firearms handling, crowd management, anti-terrorism operations, criminal investigation, physical conditioning, and advanced policing techniques
  • Medical Coverage: Comprehensive healthcare through Central Government Health Scheme covering police personnel and family members with cashless treatment at authorized hospitals
  • Pension & Family Security: Defined pension scheme ensuring post-retirement financial security with family pension provisions protecting dependents after officer's demise
  • Leave Entitlements: Earned leave, casual leave, medical leave, special leave for emergencies, and casual duty provisions balancing operational requirements with work-life balance

Job Responsibilities and Career Growth: Delhi Police Head Constables (AWO) perform armed escort duties protecting high-profile personalities, VIP protection during official events, armed surveillance operations, and specialized counter-terrorism tasks in collaboration with armed units. TPO (Trainee Police Officers) handle general policing duties including traffic management, patrol operations, community engagement, crime prevention, investigation support, and crowd control during public events. Head Constables advance to Senior Head Constable positions after 8-10 years gaining salary increments and supervisory responsibilities leading constabulary teams at police stations or specialized units. Candidates can specialize in various police wings including Crime Branch, Traffic Police, VIP Protection Unit, Cyber Crime, or Special Operations Units based on performance and aptitude. The position offers opportunities to work in India's largest metropolitan police force, handle complex security challenges, contribute to national capital's safety and security maintenance, and serve millions of Delhi residents throughout distinguished 35+ year police service tenures combining professional challenges with national pride.

Understanding SSC's Role and Complete Police Head Constable Recruitment Process Timeline

The Staff Selection Commission, established in 1975 and headquartered in New Delhi, serves as the premier recruitment agency conducting examinations for Group-B and Group-C positions across Indian government. SSC operates through regional offices across all major cities conducting recruitment annually for various posts including clerical staff, constables, constabulary officers, and specialized positions for Central Armed Police Forces, Delhi Police, and other organizations. The Commission maintains highest standards of examination integrity, fairness, transparency, and merit-based selection throughout recruitment cycles ensuring selected candidates possess requisite competence, character, and commitment to public service and national security objectives.
