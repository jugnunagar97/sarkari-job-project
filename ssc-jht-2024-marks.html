Mode Of Selection

The selection process for the SSC Junior Hindi Translator (JHT) recruitment includes the following steps:

  • Written Exam (Tier-1): An objective or multiple-choice exam to test the candidate’s basic knowledge, aptitude, or subject-specific skills. Often acts as the preliminary screening stage.
  • Subjective Written Exam (Tier-2): A descriptive exam where candidates may be required to write essays, solve detailed problems, or answer subjective questions related to the job or subject matter. This stage evaluates deeper understanding, writing ability, and problem-solving skills.
  • Document Verification (DV): A process where candidates are required to present and verify their educational, personal, and professional documents. This ensures the candidate meets eligibility criteria and has provided authentic information.
  • Medical Examination (ME): A physical fitness test to confirm that the candidate meets the required medical standards for the position. Common for jobs requiring physical fitness.
