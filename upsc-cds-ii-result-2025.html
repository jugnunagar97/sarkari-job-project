Why UPSC CDS-II Result 2025 Matters for Defense Career Aspirants

Why UPSC CDS II Result 2025 Represents Gateway to Officer Careers

The UPSC CDS-II Result 2025, declared on 09 October 2025, marks a crucial milestone for candidates aspiring to join India's Armed Forces as commissioned officers. This result determines eligibility for the prestigious SSB interview process across 453 vacancies in Indian Military Academy, Air Force Academy, Indian Naval Academy, and Officers Training Academy.

A total of 9,085 candidates have qualified from the written examination conducted on 14 September 2025, creating a competitive selection ratio. The result has been released in PDF format containing both roll numbers and names of successful candidates for transparent verification.

Key Result Highlights:

  • 9,085 candidates shortlisted for SSB interview stage from lakhs of applicants
  • Result available in roll number-wise and name-wise PDF formats for easy searching
  • Qualified candidates must prepare for 5-day rigorous SSB assessment and medical examination
  • Final selection based on combined performance in written exam, SSB interview, and medical fitness

Selected candidates will undergo comprehensive officer training at respective academies, developing leadership skills, military tactics, and professional competencies essential for commanding India's defense forces.

Understanding CDS Examination Pattern and SSB Interview Process

Complete Guide to CDS Selection Process and Services Selection Board Assessment

The CDS selection process comprises multiple rigorous stages ensuring only the most capable candidates receive officer commissions. The written examination consists of three papers: English (100 marks), General Knowledge (100 marks), and Elementary Mathematics (100 marks for IMA, INA, AFA candidates).

OTA candidates appear for only English and General Knowledge papers totaling 200 marks. The examination tests aptitude, reasoning ability, current affairs knowledge, and mathematical skills essential for officer-level decision-making in military operations.

SSB Interview Components:

  • Stage I: Officer Intelligence Rating Test and Picture Perception & Description Test (screening stage)
  • Stage II: Psychological Tests including Thematic Apperception Test, Word Association Test, and Situation Reaction Test
  • Group Testing: Group discussions, group planning exercises, progressive group tasks, and lecturette assessing teamwork
  • Personal Interview: Comprehensive assessment of personality, motivation, leadership potential, and general awareness

Candidates clearing SSB undergo medical examination assessing physical fitness, vision standards, and overall health parameters specific to each service. Final merit lists combine written exam marks and SSB conference ratings determining academy allocation.

About UPSC Organization and Combined Defence Services Framework

Understanding UPSC's Role and CDS Examination Legacy in Officer Recruitment

The Union Public Service Commission conducts CDS examinations twice annually, selecting candidates for tri-service officer training at India's premier military academies. UPSC maintains transparent merit-based selection processes ensuring the finest graduates join armed forces leadership cadres.

The Combined Defence Services Examination, established in 1948, stands as one of India's oldest competitive examinations for officer recruitment. CDS has produced thousands of distinguished officers including Chiefs of Army, Navy, and Air Force who served India with distinction.

CDS Examination Framework:

  • Conducted twice yearly (CDS-I in February and CDS-II in September) providing multiple opportunities
  • Open to male and female candidates for most entries including IMA, INA, AFA, and OTA
  • Comprehensive assessment combining written aptitude, psychological evaluation, and physical fitness standards
  • Final selection considers academic merit, SSB performance, medical fitness, and vacancy availability

Joining through CDS provides opportunities to serve at India's borders, lead troops in challenging environments, and contribute to national security while building distinguished military careers with honor, adventure, and professional growth.
