How To Check & Download UPSSSC Auditor Mains Result 2025
  • Visit official UPSSSC website at upsssc.gov.in from desktop or mobile browser.
  • On homepage, find section titled "Mains Result" or check under "Important News & Alerts" section.
  • Select relevant link for "Assistant Accountant & Auditor Main Exam 2025 Result".
  • Click on result link redirecting to candidate login portal page.
  • Enter your Registration Number, Enrollment Number or Roll Number in first field.
  • Enter Date of Birth in DD/MM/YYYY format in second field for verification.
  • Fill captcha code displayed on screen correctly to proceed further.
  • Click on "Submit" or "See Result" button to view your mains examination result.
  • Result PDF will open displaying your Roll Number, Name, Category, Marks Obtained, Qualifying Status.
  • Download result PDF and save multiple copies for document verification stage.
  • Take printout of result for submission during document verification process at district headquarters.

Auditor & Accountant: UP's Financial Management Professionals

Filling 1,828 Positions Managing Government Accounts Across Uttar Pradesh Departments

The UPSSSC Auditor and Assistant Accountant Recruitment 2024 fills 1,828 critical financial management positions across various Uttar Pradesh Government departments. These professionals handle government accounting, financial auditing, budget management, expenditure control, revenue accounting, tax compliance, and financial reporting managing state's thousands of crores budget ensuring transparency, accountability, fiscal discipline serving UP's 24 crore+ population across 75 districts. Auditors conduct internal audits, verify financial records, detect irregularities, recommend improvements while Assistant Accountants maintain accounts, prepare financial statements, process payments, manage tax collections contributing efficient financial administration ensuring judicious use of public funds supporting state's development vision under various departments including Finance, Revenue, PWD, Education, Health, Agriculture ensuring fiscal governance excellence.

Role-Wise Job Responsibilities:

  • Auditor (383 posts): Internal audit, financial verification, compliance checking, fraud detection, audit reports, recommendation implementation
  • Assistant Accountant General (668 posts): General accounting, financial records, payment processing, budget monitoring, expenditure tracking, account reconciliation
  • Assistant Accountant Special (950 posts): Specialized accounting for specific departments, tax accounting, fund accounting, project accounting
  • Treasury management: Cash flow management, bank reconciliation, fund transfers, investment tracking, liquidity management
  • Financial reporting: Monthly, quarterly, annual reports, statistical statements, budget variance analysis, performance indicators

Salary and Benefits:

  • Pay Level 6: ₹35,400-₹1,12,400 per month (7th Pay Commission) for Assistant Accountant and Auditor
  • Gross in-hand: ₹40,000-₹50,000/month initially (basic + DA + HRA + TA + other allowances)
  • Annual CTC: ₹6-7 lakh initially including all components and perquisites

UP Government accountants enjoy comprehensive state benefits including DA (50%), HRA, transport allowance, medical facilities, GPF, pension, LTC, children education allowance, promotion opportunities to Senior Accountant, Deputy Chief Accountant levels ensuring stable financial careers.

Mains Result Analysis: Exam Pattern and Expected Cutoff

Result Released 17 October 2025 for Exam Conducted 16 February 2025

The UPSSSC Auditor Mains Result 2025 announced qualified candidates for document verification stage selecting 1,828 candidates based on merit performance in written examination. Released on 17 October 2025 for examination conducted 16 February, the result includes category-wise merit lists with roll numbers of successful candidates who scored above cutoff marks in competitive examination. Qualified candidates must prepare original documents including B.Com degree, PET 2023 certificate, O Level certificate, caste certificate, domicile certificate for verification at district headquarters ensuring final selection to prestigious accounting positions in UP Government departments managing state's financial operations.

Mains Exam Pattern:

  • Financial Accounting: 40 questions covering accounting principles, double entry system, journal, ledger, trial balance, final accounts
  • Cost Accounting: 30 questions testing costing methods, cost sheets, budgeting, variance analysis, cost control
  • Auditing: 30 questions on audit principles, internal audit, statutory audit, vouching, verification, audit reports
  • Commercial Laws: 25 questions covering Companies Act, Income Tax, GST, negotiable instruments, contract act
  • Computer Knowledge: 25 questions testing MS Office, Tally, internet, computer fundamentals, cyber security
  • Total questions: 150 Multiple Choice Questions across five sections
  • Total marks: 150 marks (each question carries 1 mark)
  • Duration: 2 hours (120 minutes) for completing examination
  • Negative marking: 0.25 marks (1/4th mark) deducted for each wrong answer
  • Exam mode: Offline OMR-based examination conducted at centers across Uttar Pradesh

Marks Calculation Method:

  • Formula: Total Score = (Correct Answers × 1) - (Wrong Answers × 0.25)
  • Example: 110 correct + 25 wrong + 15 unattempted = (110 × 1) - (25 × 0.25) = 110 - 6.25 = 103.75 marks
  • Percentage calculation: (Total Score / 150) × 100 = Percentage achieved
  • Category-wise selection: Separate merit lists for General, OBC, SC, ST, EWS categories

Expected Cutoff Marks (Category-Wise):

  • General/UR category: 95-105/150 (63-70% marks) due to high competition for accounting positions
  • EWS category: 90-100/150 (60-67% marks) slight relaxation from general cutoff
  • OBC category: 85-95/150 (57-63% marks) moderate relaxation for backward classes
  • SC category: 75-85/150 (50-57% marks) substantial relaxation ensuring representation
  • ST category: 70-80/150 (47-53% marks) highest relaxation for tribal candidates
  • PwBD category: 65-75/150 (43-50% marks) special consideration for persons with disabilities
  • Post-wise variation: Auditor posts may have higher cutoffs than Assistant Accountant due to responsibility level
  • Cutoff varies: Final cutoffs depend on overall performance, difficulty level, vacancies in each category

Candidates scoring above expected cutoffs must prepare for document verification stage scheduled November-December 2025 at respective district headquarters bringing all original certificates for scrutiny ensuring final selection to UP Government accounting positions.

Career Progression: Accountant to Chief Accounts Officer

₹40,000 Starting Salary with Promotions to Senior Financial Management Positions

The UP Government accounting career offers excellent progression path advancing from entry-level accountant to senior financial management positions. Professionals gain valuable experience in government accounting systems, financial management, audit procedures, budget management, and qualify for promotions to Senior Accountant, Accounts Officer, Deputy Chief Accountant, Chief Accounts Officer levels managing departmental finances, supervising accounting staff, ensuring fiscal compliance contributing state's financial governance serving various government departments managing crores of rupees ensuring transparency, accountability, efficient fund utilization supporting Uttar Pradesh's development programs, welfare schemes ensuring judicious use of taxpayer money.

Career Advancement Path:

  • Assistant Accountant/Auditor (0-5 years): Entry level handling accounts, payments, receipts, audits, financial records maintenance
  • Senior Accountant (5-10 years): Complex accounting, financial statements, budget preparation, junior staff supervision
  • Accounts Officer (10-15 years): Section management, financial oversight, audit coordination, policy implementation
  • Deputy Chief Accountant (15-20 years): Department-level financial management, strategic planning, senior decision-making
  • Chief Accounts Officer (20-25 years): Heading financial operations, policy formulation, district/regional financial management

Job Responsibilities and Work Profile:

  • Accounting duties: Recording transactions, maintaining ledgers, preparing trial balance, final accounts, reconciliation statements
  • Payment processing: Bill verification, payment authorization, salary processing, pension payments, vendor payments
  • Budget management: Budget allocation, expenditure monitoring, variance analysis, reappropriation proposals, budget reports
  • Audit support: Responding to audit queries, providing documents, implementing audit recommendations, corrective actions
  • Tax compliance: TDS deduction, GST compliance, income tax returns, professional tax, other statutory compliances
  • Financial reporting: Monthly accounts, quarterly reports, annual financial statements, statistical returns, performance indicators

Comprehensive Benefits Package:

  • Basic salary: ₹35,400-₹1,12,400/month (Pay Level 6) with regular annual increments
  • Dearness Allowance: Currently 50% of basic (revised biannually based on inflation)
  • House Rent Allowance: 8-24% of basic depending on posting location (Rural/Urban/Metro)
  • Transport Allowance: ₹3,600-₹7,200/month for official travel, commuting expenses
  • Medical facilities: Government hospital treatment + reimbursement for self and family
  • GPF accumulation: General Provident Fund with interest building retirement corpus tax-free
  • Pension: Monthly pension after retirement ensuring post-retirement financial security
  • LTC: Leave Travel Concession covering family travel expenses biannually
  • Children education: Education allowance for children's schooling expenses
  • Professional development: CA/CMA coaching support, certification courses, training programs, conferences

Work Environment and Culture:

  • Working hours: 10:00 AM - 5:00 PM typically (Monday-Friday) with month-end extended hours
  • Posting locations: District headquarters, divisional offices, state secretariat across 75 districts of UP
  • Transfer policy: Transfers every 3-5 years ensuring diverse experience across departments, locations
  • Work pressure: Month-end closing, year-end accounts, audit periods requiring extra effort, dedication
  • Professional environment: Formal office culture, financial discipline, ethical practices, accountability

UP Government Auditor and Assistant Accountant positions provide rewarding careers combining job satisfaction of financial management, stable employment, excellent compensation, comprehensive benefits, promotion opportunities to senior positions making accounting service attractive profession for B.Com graduates.

UPSSSC: Managing UP's Subordinate Services Recruitment Since 1974

Conducting 50+ Annual Examinations Filling 20,000+ State Government Positions

The Uttar Pradesh Subordinate Service Selection Commission (UPSSSC) established 1974 serves as premier recruitment authority conducting examinations for Group C posts across UP State Government departments. UPSSSC ensures merit-based transparent selection for accounting positions, clerical staff, revenue officers, technical personnel, and other subordinate positions serving state's 24 crore+ population across 75 districts maintaining high examination standards, fair evaluation processes, efficient recruitment procedures ensuring quality workforce for Uttar Pradesh's governance, development, financial administration contributing state's progress managing India's most populous state ensuring administrative excellence under constitutional mandate providing employment opportunities to lakhs of educated youth annually.

Major UPSSSC Examinations:

  • PET: Preliminary Eligibility Test mandatory qualifying exam for all Group C posts in UP Government
  • Accounting Posts: Auditor, Assistant Accountant recruitment for Finance Department, other departments
  • Revenue Posts: Revenue Inspector, Lekhpal (VDO) managing land records, revenue collection across districts
  • Clerical Posts: Junior Assistant, Junior Clerk, Assistant Grade III for administrative departments
  • Technical Posts: Junior Engineer (Civil, Electrical, Mechanical), Draftsman supporting infrastructure projects
  • Other Posts: Forest Guard, Agriculture Assistant, Statistical Officer, Computer Operator recruitment

  • Departmental accounts: Each government department maintaining separate accounts sections managing allocations

Accounting Software and Systems:
