UPSSSC Draftsman Career Pathway in UP's Irrigation Sector

From Entry-Level Drafting to Senior Engineering Roles with 283 Vacancies

The UPSSSC Draftsman & Cartographer recruitment 2025 (Advt 11/2023) fills 283 positions (250 Draftsmen, 33 Cartographers) in Uttar Pradesh's Irrigation & Water Resources Department, addressing 15% vacancy in canal mapping and flood control projects across Ganga basin (serving 2.4 crore hectares). Draftsmen create technical drawings for dams like Tehri (capacity 4 million acre-feet), using AutoCAD for hydraulic designs aligned with Jal Jeevan Mission (₹20,000 crore allocation 2025-26). Cartographers specialize in GIS mapping for watershed management in Bundelkhand (drought-prone, 40% arid land), integrating drone surveys for real-time flood alerts under Namami Gange. Eligibility requires diploma in civil engineering/architecture from AICTE-approved institutes like UPSEE affiliates, with 50% marks (45% for reserved). Pay scale: Level-6 (₹35,400-₹1,12,400 basic + 50% DA Oct 2025, HRA 8-27% urban/rural) grossing ₹55,000-₹70,000 monthly, plus perks: NPS (10% contribution), medical (₹5 lakh CGHS), LTC (yearly home travel). Progression: Junior Draftsman (0-5 years) → Senior Draftsman (Level-7, ₹44,900-₹1,42,400 via departmental exam) → Assistant Engineer (Level-10, ₹56,100-₹1,77,500 after AMIE/B.Tech bridge, 8-12 years) → Executive Engineer (Level-12, ₹78,800-₹2,09,200) → Chief Engineer (apex Level-14, ₹1,44,200-₹2,18,200, 20+ years). Training: 3-month induction at Irrigation Academy Lucknow on BIM software, safety protocols for 500+ km canal networks. Postings: 70% rural in districts like Gorakhpur (flood risk), with 5-year transfers. This supports UP's water security goal, employing 1.2 lakh in sector.

Mastering UPSSSC Draftsman Exam: Technical Drawing & GIS Focus

100-MCQ OMR Test on 23 Nov 2025 with 1/3 Negative Marking Strategy

The UPSSSC Draftsman & Cartographer exam 2025 (16 Nov per notice, but official update 23 Nov) features 100 objective questions (100 marks, 2 hours) at 200+ centers in Lucknow, Kanpur zones—covering Technical Drawing (30 marks: orthographic projections, sectional views per IS 696), Surveying & Mapping (25 marks: chain/tape methods, contouring for UP's 1,000+ rivers), Civil Engineering Basics (20 marks: RCC designs, soil mechanics for embankments), General Knowledge (15 marks: UP irrigation schemes like Saryu Canal), Reasoning/Quant (10 marks: basic aptitude). Marking: +1 correct, -1/3 wrong; target 70-80 attempts (85% accuracy) for 65+ cutoff (gen: 60, OBC: 55, SC/ST: 50 historical). Cartographer variant adds GIS/RS (10 marks: ArcGIS tools, satellite imagery from ISRO Bhuvan). Prep: Practice 15 mocks on OMR sheets, revise diploma syllabus (UPBTE notes), focus high-yield: Scales/projections (20%), hydrology (UP-specific: Yamuna floods). Admit card (7-10 days prior) requires registration no./DOB; carry photo ID. Post-exam: PET (running 1.6km men/800m women in 8 min) for physical fitness in field surveys. Merit via normalized scores; DV checks diploma originals. Use UPSSSC app for GK on policies (e.g., Atal Bhujal Yojana). With 50,000 applicants, cutoffs rise 5-10%; prioritize accuracy over speed.
