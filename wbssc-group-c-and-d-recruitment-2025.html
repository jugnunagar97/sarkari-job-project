WBSSC Group C & D: Non-Teaching Support Staff in West Bengal Government Schools

Transforming 8477 Candidates into Government School Support Staff Across West Bengal

The WBSSC Group C and D Recruitment 2025 fills 8,477 non-teaching staff positions in government-aided and sponsored high/higher secondary schools across West Bengal. Group C Clerks manage administrative work, record keeping, and office correspondence while Group D staff (Peons, Laboratory Attendants, Night Guards) provide essential support services ensuring smooth functioning of 10,000+ government schools serving crores of students statewide.

Post-Wise Breakdown:

  • Group C Clerk (2,989 posts—35%): Office administration, file management, correspondence, record maintenance
  • Group D Staff (5,488 posts—65%): Peons (file movement, assistance), Lab Attendants (laboratory support), Night Guards (security)

Salary and Benefits:

  • Group C (Clerk): ₹22,700 - ₹26,000 per month (Pay Band-2)
  • Group D Staff: ₹20,050 per month base salary
  • Additional benefits: DA (currently 50%), HRA, medical facilities, pension
  • Gross in-hand: Group C (₹35,000-₹40,000), Group D (₹30,000-₹35,000) with allowances

WBSSC Group C and D employees enjoy West Bengal government benefits including medical coverage, GPF, pension, LTC, festival allowances ensuring stable career for 8th/10th pass candidates with promotion opportunities to senior positions within 10-15 years of service.

1st State Level Selection Test: Written Exam and Interview for Merit-Based Selection

Computer-Based Examination with Region-Wise Vacancy Allocation Across West Bengal

The WBSSC 1st SLST 2025 follows transparent selection process with written examination testing general knowledge, reasoning, and subject-specific skills followed by interview/skill test for shortlisted candidates. This marks first state-level selection test after 7 years providing employment opportunities to thousands of candidates across 23 districts of West Bengal.

Written Examination Pattern:

  • Mode: Computer-based test (CBT) or OMR-based depending on group
  • Duration: 90-120 minutes (varies by post)
  • Subjects covered: General Knowledge, General Awareness, Reasoning, Mathematics, English/Bengali
  • Question type: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)
  • Negative marking: As per official notification (typically 0.25 marks per wrong answer)

Selection Process Stages:

  • Stage I: Written Examination (qualifying + merit basis)
  • Stage II: Skill Test/Interview for shortlisted candidates (Group C may have typing test)
  • Stage III: Document Verification checking educational certificates, caste certificates, age proof
  • Stage IV: Medical Examination ensuring physical fitness for duties
  • Final merit: Based on written exam marks (interview marks may be added as per rules)

Expected Syllabus Coverage:

  • General Knowledge: West Bengal history, geography, culture, current affairs, government schemes
  • General Awareness: National and international current events, sports, awards, science developments
  • Reasoning: Logical reasoning, analytical ability, problem-solving, pattern recognition
  • Mathematics: Arithmetic, percentages, profit-loss, time-distance (8th/10th level based on post)
  • Language: Basic English/Bengali grammar, comprehension, vocabulary

Region-Wise Vacancy Distribution:

  • Kolkata Region: Maximum vacancies (urban concentration of schools)
  • North Bengal: Significant allocation (Darjeeling, Jalpaiguri, Cooch Behar, Alipurduar districts)
  • South Bengal: Districts like Howrah, Hooghly, 24 Parganas with substantial vacancies
  • West Bengal proper: Bankura, Purulia, Birbhum, Murshidabad regions
  • Candidates can check detailed region-wise vacancy distribution in official notification

Candidates advised to prepare from 8th/10th standard West Bengal Board textbooks for respective groups, current affairs from last 12 months, and practice previous WBSSC exam papers ensuring comprehensive preparation for competitive examination.

Career Growth and Work Environment in West Bengal Government Schools

₹20,050 to ₹26,000 Starting Salary with Government Benefits and Job Security

The WBSSC Group C and D career offers stable West Bengal government employment in educational institutions providing essential support services. Clerks manage school administration, student records, accounts while Group D staff assist in laboratories, maintain cleanliness, provide security ensuring smooth school operations serving lakhs of students across West Bengal.

Detailed Salary Breakup:

  • Group C Clerk: Basic ₹22,700-₹26,000 + DA ₹11,350-₹13,000 (50%) + HRA ₹2,270-₹2,600 (10%) = ₹36,320-₹41,600/month
  • Group D Staff: Basic ₹20,050 + DA ₹10,025 (50%) + HRA ₹2,005 (10%) = ₹32,080/month
  • Additional allowances: Transport allowance, special allowance, festival bonus
  • Annual increment: 3% of basic pay each year

Post-Wise Responsibilities:

  • Group C Clerk: Maintaining student admission registers, preparing salary bills, handling school correspondence, managing stock registers, assisting headmaster in administrative work
  • Peon: File movement between departments, delivering notices, assisting in office work, maintaining cleanliness
  • Laboratory Attendant: Setting up experiments, maintaining lab equipment, assisting science teachers, ensuring lab safety
  • Night Guard: School security during night hours, preventing theft/damage, maintaining vigilance

Career Progression:

  • Group C Clerk (0-5 years): Entry-level clerical work under supervision
  • Senior Clerk (5-10 years): Independent handling of administrative sections
  • Head Clerk/Office Superintendent (10-15 years): Supervising clerical staff, complete office administration
  • Group D Staff: Limited vertical promotion but can apply for higher posts through competitive exams

Work Environment:

  • Location: Government-aided/sponsored high schools, higher secondary schools across West Bengal
  • Duty hours: Group C (10 AM - 5 PM school hours), Group D (varies—day/night shifts for guards)
  • Work atmosphere: Educational institution environment, interaction with teachers, students, parents
  • School calendar: Work during academic session, holidays during school vacations (summer/winter breaks)

Comprehensive Government Benefits:

  • Medical facilities: Free treatment at government hospitals + medical reimbursement (₹3-5 lakh limit)
  • Leave benefits: 30 days earned leave + 10 days casual leave + 20 days medical leave annually
  • General Provident Fund: Monthly contribution with 7.1% annual interest
  • Pension: 50% of last drawn salary after 20 years service
  • Gratuity: ₹10-20 lakh on retirement depending on years of service
  • Festival allowances: Durga Puja bonus, annual increment, performance-linked incentives

WBSSC Group C and D employees contribute to West Bengal's education system supporting academic activities, maintaining infrastructure, ensuring security while enjoying government employment stability and comprehensive benefits for 8th/10th pass candidates.
